# Win/Loss Analysis Framework

## Purpose
A systematic approach to understanding why deals are won or lost. Win/loss analysis is the single highest-ROI research activity a marketing team can do — it directly improves positioning, messaging, sales enablement, and product roadmap decisions.

---

## Part 1: The Win/Loss Interview

### Who to Interview

| Source | Insight Quality | Access Difficulty | When to Use |
|--------|:-:|:-:|-------------|
| **Won customer** | High | Easy | Always — understand why they chose you |
| **Lost prospect** | Highest | Medium | Always — understand why they didn't |
| **Churned customer** | Very High | Medium | Quarterly — understand why they left |
| **Sales rep (internal)** | Medium | Easy | Every deal — quick internal debrief |
| **Champion who lost internally** | Very High | Hard | When possible — understand internal buying dynamics |

### Interview Guide: Won Deals

| Question | What It Reveals |
|----------|----------------|
| "Walk me through how you first became aware of [problem]." | Trigger events, timing |
| "What solutions did you evaluate?" | Competitive set |
| "What were your top 3 criteria for choosing?" | Decision drivers |
| "What almost made you NOT choose us?" | Hidden objections, close-call risks |
| "Who else was involved in the decision?" | Buying committee, internal dynamics |
| "What was the moment you decided we were the right choice?" | Decision triggers, proof points that worked |
| "How would you describe us to a colleague?" | Real positioning (in their words) |
| "What's one thing we could improve about the buying process?" | Sales experience gaps |

### Interview Guide: Lost Deals

| Question | What It Reveals |
|----------|----------------|
| "Walk me through your evaluation process." | How they buy, who's involved |
| "What were your must-have criteria?" | Requirements we may not meet |
| "At what point did you decide not to go with us?" | Where we lose in the journey |
| "What did [winner] offer that we didn't?" | Competitive gaps |
| "Was there anything that would have changed your decision?" | Fixable vs. structural issues |
| "How would you describe us vs. [who you chose]?" | Perceived positioning gap |
| "What was your biggest concern about us?" | Objections we're not handling |
| "Would you consider us in the future? Under what conditions?" | Re-engagement opportunities |

## Part 4: Turning Insights into Action

### The Win/Loss → Action Pipeline

| Insight Source | Where It Goes | Who Owns | Timeline |
|---------------|-------------|---------|----------|
| Winning messages | Battle cards + sales scripts | Product Marketing | 1 week |
| Losing reasons (product) | Product roadmap input | Product Marketing → Product | Quarterly |
| Losing reasons (messaging) | Positioning refresh | Strategy + PMM | 2 weeks |
| Competitor intelligence | Battle cards + competitive page | Product Marketing | 1 week |
| Pricing feedback | Pricing review | Strategy + Finance | Monthly |
| Sales process issues | Sales playbook updates | Sales Enablement | 2 weeks |
| Decision criteria patterns | Landing page + content updates | Content + PMM | 2 weeks |

### Quarterly Win/Loss Report

| Section | Contents |
|---------|---------|
| **Executive Summary** | Win rate trends, top 3 win reasons, top 3 loss reasons |
| **Competitive Landscape** | Win rates by competitor, emerging trends |
| **Product Gaps** | Features requested by lost prospects, prioritized |
| **Messaging Opportunities** | Language that wins, language that loses |
| **Process Improvements** | Sales experience feedback, buying process friction |
| **Recommendations** | Top 5 actions for next quarter |

## Part 5: Win/Loss Program Setup

### Getting Started Checklist

- [ ] Designate a win/loss owner (usually Product Marketing)
- [ ] Set interview targets: minimum 3 wins + 3 losses per quarter
- [ ] Create standard interview guides (use templates above)
- [ ] Build the tracking spreadsheet or CRM fields
- [ ] Establish a process for sharing insights (monthly review meeting)
- [ ] Get sales buy-in — they need to facilitate introductions to lost prospects

### Cadence

| Activity | Frequency | Owner |
|----------|:---------:|-------|
| Win/loss interviews | Ongoing (min 6/quarter) | PMM or designated interviewer |
| Deal-level logging | Within 1 week of close (won or lost) | Sales rep + PMM |
| Pattern analysis | Monthly | PMM |
| Quarterly report | Quarterly | PMM → Leadership |
| Battle card updates | Quarterly (or after major insight) | PMM |
| Product roadmap input | Quarterly | PMM → Product |
