# Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Framework

## Purpose
A structured template for conducting Quarterly Business Reviews with clients or internal stakeholders. QBRs are the single most important retention and expansion tool — they demonstrate value, align on strategy, and surface opportunities.

---

## Part 1: QBR Preparation

### Pre-QBR Checklist

| Task | Owner | Deadline | Done |
|------|-------|:--------:|:----:|
| Pull all performance data for the quarter | Analyst / Account Manager | QBR -7 days | |
| Draft the QBR deck | Account Manager + Strategist | QBR -5 days | |
| Internal review of deck with team lead | Account Director | QBR -3 days | |
| Send pre-read / agenda to client | Account Manager | QBR -2 days | |
| Prepare 3 strategic recommendations for next quarter | Strategist | QBR -2 days | |
| Identify expansion / upsell opportunity (if appropriate) | Account Director | QBR -2 days | |

## Part 3: QBR Facilitation Guide

### Opening (5 min)
- Thank the client for their time
- Set the tone: "This is a strategic conversation, not just a report"
- Confirm the agenda and time

### Presenting Results (20 min)
- Lead with wins — build confidence before discussing challenges
- For every challenge, present the learning AND the adaptation
- Use visuals: trends, charts, before/after comparisons
- Pause for questions after each section

### Strategic Discussion (20 min)
- Present recommendations with clear rationale
- Ask the client: "What's changing in your business that we should factor in?"
- Co-create next quarter's priorities (don't just tell them)
- If expansion opportunity exists, present it as a natural evolution

### Closing (15 min)
- Summarize agreed next steps
- Confirm next QBR date
- Ask: "On a scale of 1-10, how satisfied are you with our partnership?"
- Ask: "What's one thing we could do better?"

## Part 5: QBR Best Practices

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Lead with business impact, not activity metrics | Don't list every task you completed |
| Be honest about what didn't work | Don't hide bad results — clients always find out |
| Bring strategic recommendations, not just reports | Don't just present data without insight |
| Ask about their evolving business priorities | Don't assume last quarter's goals still apply |
| Present expansion as solving a problem, not an upsell | Don't pitch mid-QBR without strategic context |
| Send a pre-read so the QBR is discussion, not presentation | Don't use QBR time to present information they could read |
