# SOW / Contract Templates

## Purpose
Standard templates for Statements of Work and service agreements. These protect both the agency and the client by clearly defining scope, deliverables, timelines, payment terms, and boundaries.

---

## Part 1: Statement of Work (SOW) Template

### SOW Header

```
STATEMENT OF WORK

Agreement between:
  [Agency Legal Name] ("Agency")
  [Client Legal Name] ("Client")

Effective Date: [Date]
SOW Number: [SOW-YYYY-###]
Reference Agreement: [Master Service Agreement #, if applicable]
```

### Section 7: Intellectual Property

| IP Type | Ownership |
|---------|----------|
| **Final deliverables** | Client owns all final, approved deliverables upon full payment |

| **Client data** | Client retains all ownership of their data, brand assets, and proprietary information |
| **Third-party licenses** | Stock photos, fonts, software — client responsibility unless specified |
| **Portfolio usage** | Agency may reference the engagement in marketing materials unless client opts out |

### Section 8: Confidentiality

Both parties agree to:
- Maintain confidentiality of proprietary information shared during the engagement
- Not disclose confidential information to third parties without written consent
- Return or destroy confidential materials upon termination
- Confidentiality survives termination for [2] years

---

### Section 9: Change Order Process

For any work outside the defined scope:

1. **Request:** Either party submits a change request in writing
2. **Estimate:** Agency provides scope, timeline, and cost estimate within [3] business days
3. **Approval:** Client provides written approval before work begins
4. **Execution:** Work proceeds per approved change order terms
5. **Documentation:** Change order becomes an amendment to this SOW

## Part 2: SOW Checklist

### Before Signing, Verify:

- [ ] Scope is clearly defined (no ambiguous deliverables)
- [ ] Out-of-scope items are explicitly listed
- [ ] Payment terms and amounts are correct
- [ ] Termination clause is reasonable for both parties
- [ ] IP ownership is clear
- [ ] Client responsibilities and approval SLAs are defined
- [ ] Change order process is documented
- [ ] Both parties' legal names and authorized signers are correct
- [ ] Start date and end date are confirmed
- [ ] Any special terms from negotiation are included

---

## Part 3: Common SOW Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Risk | Prevention |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Vague scope ("marketing support") | Endless scope creep | List specific deliverables with quantities |
| No approval SLA | Client delays stall projects | Define turnaround time with delay clause |
| No change order process | Free work on "small additions" | Formal change order for anything not in scope |
| No kill fee | Client cancels after you've staffed up | Include early termination fee |
| IP before payment | Delivering work without getting paid | IP transfers upon full payment |
| No media spend cap | Client disputes ad spend amounts | Monthly cap with written approval for increases |
