# Marketing-Sales SLA Template

A Service Level Agreement between marketing and sales that defines lead handoff criteria, response times, and mutual accountability. Even solo founders benefit from this — it forces clarity on what a "qualified lead" actually looks like and what happens after someone raises their hand.

---

## 1. Definitions

### Lead Stages

| Stage | Definition | Owner | Criteria |
|-------|-----------|:-----:|---------|
| **Visitor** | Anonymous website/content visitor | Marketing | Has engaged with content but not identified |
| **Lead** | Known contact who shared info | Marketing | Submitted form, signed up for newsletter, downloaded resource |
| **MQL** (Marketing Qualified Lead) | Lead who meets qualification criteria | Marketing | Meets scoring threshold (see below) |
| **SQL** (Sales Qualified Lead) | MQL accepted by sales as worth pursuing | Sales | Confirmed fit through initial conversation |
| **Opportunity** | SQL with active deal in pipeline | Sales | Budget, authority, need, timeline confirmed |
| **Customer** | Closed deal | Sales → Success | Payment received |

### Lead Scoring Model

| Action | Points | Category |
|--------|:------:|----------|
| Visited pricing page | +15 | Intent |
| Downloaded lead magnet | +10 | Engagement |
| Opened 3+ emails | +5 | Engagement |
| Clicked email CTA | +10 | Intent |
| Attended webinar | +15 | Engagement |
| Requested demo/consultation | +25 | Intent |
| Visited site 3+ times in 7 days | +10 | Engagement |
| Matches ICP company size | +10 | Fit |
| Matches ICP role/title | +10 | Fit |
| Matches ICP industry | +5 | Fit |

**MQL Threshold:** _____ points (recommended: 40-50)

## 2. Marketing Commitments

### What Marketing Will Deliver

| Commitment | Target | Measurement |
|-----------|:------:|-------------|
| **MQLs per month** | # | CRM/marketing automation count |
| **Lead quality** (MQL → SQL conversion rate) | >___% | Sales feedback loop |
| **Lead response time** (form → first email) | <___ minutes | Automation timestamp |
| **Content support** (case studies, one-pagers) | # per quarter | Sales team feedback |
| **Campaign calendar shared** | By ___ of each month | Calendar published |

### Lead Handoff Requirements

When passing an MQL to sales, marketing provides:

- [ ] Contact info (name, email, company, role)
- [ ] Lead score and scoring breakdown
- [ ] Content engagement history (what they downloaded/viewed)
- [ ] Source/channel (how they found us)
- [ ] ICP fit assessment
- [ ] Any known pain points or questions (from form fields, chat, etc.)

## 3. Sales Commitments

### What Sales Will Deliver

| Commitment | Target | Measurement |
|-----------|:------:|-------------|
| **MQL follow-up time** | <___ hours | CRM timestamp |
| **Follow-up attempts** (before marking unresponsive) | ≥___ attempts over ___ days | CRM activity log |
| **MQL disposition** (accept/reject with reason) | Within ___ business days | CRM status update |
| **Win/loss feedback** | Within ___ days of outcome | CRM notes + monthly review |
| **Closed-loop reporting** | Monthly | Revenue attributed to marketing sources |

### Sales Follow-Up Sequence

| Attempt | Timing | Channel | Action |
|:-------:|--------|---------|--------|
| 1 | Within [X] hours of MQL handoff | Email + LinkedIn | Personalized outreach referencing their engagement |
| 2 | Day 2 | Phone/video call | Direct outreach |
| 3 | Day 4 | Email | Value-add (relevant resource or insight) |
| 4 | Day 7 | LinkedIn | Social touch |
| 5 | Day 14 | Email | Final attempt with softer CTA |

## 4. Feedback Loop

### Weekly Sync (15 min)

| Agenda Item | Owner |
|------------|:-----:|
| MQL volume vs. target | Marketing |
| MQL quality feedback (top 3 best, bottom 3 worst) | Sales |
| Pipeline update from marketing leads | Sales |
| Upcoming campaigns/content that may affect lead flow | Marketing |
| Blockers or requests | Both |

## 5. Escalation Process

| Situation | Action | Escalation |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Sales not following up within SLA | Marketing flags in weekly sync | If recurring: escalate to founder/VP |
| Lead quality consistently below target | Sales provides specific feedback | Marketing adjusts scoring/targeting |
| Marketing volume below target | Marketing communicates early with plan | Adjust campaigns or expectations |
| Disagreement on lead quality | Review 10 recent MQLs together | Recalibrate scoring criteria |

---

## 6. SLA Review Cadence

| Review | Frequency | Participants | Focus |
|--------|:---------:|-------------|-------|
| Weekly sync | Weekly | Marketing + Sales leads | Tactical: this week's leads and pipeline |
| Monthly review | Monthly | Full team | Strategic: metrics, trends, adjustments |
| Quarterly SLA review | Quarterly | Leadership | Structural: update targets, scoring, definitions |

---

## Usage Notes

- **For solo founders:** Even if you're doing both marketing and sales, this template forces you to define what "qualified" means and what happens after lead capture. Use it as a self-accountability tool.
- **For small teams:** Start simple. Define MQL criteria, set follow-up times, and review weekly. Sophistication comes later.
- **Update quarterly:** Markets change, products evolve, and what constitutes a "qualified lead" shifts. Review and adjust.
