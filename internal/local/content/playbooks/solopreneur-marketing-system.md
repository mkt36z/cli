# Playbook: The Solopreneur Marketing System

## Why This Playbook Exists
Solo founders have 5-10 hours per week for marketing. Not 40. Not 20. Five to ten. This playbook is designed specifically for that constraint — it's the minimum effective dose of marketing that actually moves the needle. Everything here is calibrated for one person, no team, limited budget.

---

## The 5-Hour Weekly Marketing System

### The Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Time | Activity | Output |
|-----|:----:|----------|--------|
| **Monday** | 60 min | Content creation (write 1 post/article) | 1 pillar piece |
| **Tuesday** | 30 min | Repurpose Monday's content for social | 3-5 social posts scheduled |
| **Wednesday** | 60 min | Community engagement + outreach | 10 comments + 5 DMs |
| **Thursday** | 30 min | Email to list (nurture or value) | 1 email sent |
| **Friday** | 30 min | Review metrics + plan next week | Dashboard check + plan |

**Total: 3.5 hours/week.** This is the minimum. Add more if you have capacity.

## Content Batching System

**The 4-Hour Monthly Batch (replace daily creation with monthly batching):**

1. **Hour 1: Ideation Sprint** (30 ideas in 30 minutes)
   - Review customer questions from the month
   - Check Google Autocomplete for your main topics
   - Look at competitor content you can improve
   - Review your best-performing past content
   - Output: 30 content ideas ranked by potential impact

2. **Hour 2-3: Write 4 Pillar Pieces** (rough drafts)
   - Write fast, edit later
   - Use frameworks from Content Engine agent
   - One piece per week for the month
   - Output: 4 rough draft blog posts/articles

3. **Hour 4: Derivative Planning**
   - For each pillar piece, plan 5 derivatives:
     - 1 LinkedIn post
     - 1 X/Twitter thread
     - 1 email newsletter excerpt
     - 1 community-ready discussion starter
     - 1 quote graphic
   - Output: 20 pieces of social content planned from 4 pillar pieces

**Result:** 4 hours of work → 24 pieces of content for the month.

## The "Founder Energy" Management System

### Marketing Energy Zones

| Zone | Energy Level | Best Marketing Activities |
|------|:-----------:|--------------------------|
| **Peak** (morning, after coffee) | HIGH | Content creation, strategy, outreach |
| **Productive** (mid-morning, early afternoon) | MEDIUM | Email writing, social posting, community engagement |
| **Low** (late afternoon, after meetings) | LOW | Scheduling, analytics review, admin |
| **Recovery** (evening, weekend) | VARIABLE | Idea capture, content consumption, inspiration |

**Rule:** Never try to write content or do outreach in a low-energy zone. You'll produce mediocre work and waste time.

### The "2 Before 10" Rule
Complete your 2 most important marketing tasks before 10 AM. Before email, before Slack, before meetings. This alone will transform your marketing output.

## The Minimum Viable Marketing Funnel

For solo founders, you don't need a complex funnel. You need this:

```
CONTENT (blog/social/community)
     ↓
LEAD MAGNET (email signup)
     ↓
EMAIL NURTURE (5-7 welcome emails)
     ↓
OFFER (product/service)
     ↓
CUSTOMER SUCCESS (retention + referral)
```

**That's it.** Five steps. Everything else is optimization.

### Each Step in Detail:

**1. Content:** One piece per week. Target a keyword your ICP searches. Make it the best answer on the internet.

**2. Lead magnet:** One compelling checklist, template, or mini-course. Put a capture form on every blog post.

**3. Email nurture:** 5-7 email welcome sequence (Soap Opera style from Email Marketing Architect). Runs automatically forever.

**4. Offer:** Clear pricing page. Clear CTA. Clear value proposition. One primary action.

**5. Customer success:** Ask every customer for feedback at day 7, 30, 90. Turn happy customers into case studies and referral sources.

## The "Done > Perfect" Framework

| Perfectionist Trap | Solo Founder Reality | Action |
|-------------------|---------------------|--------|
| "My blog post needs to be perfect" | Good enough + published > perfect in drafts | Publish after 1 edit pass |
| "I need a professional video setup" | iPhone + natural light = fine | Record and publish |
| "My landing page needs better design" | Clear message > pretty design | Clarity first, beauty later |
| "I should wait until [product/feature] is ready" | Ship what you have, iterate based on feedback | Launch before you're ready |
| "I need to be on all platforms" | Master 1-2 platforms > mediocre everywhere | Pick 2, go deep |

### The 80/20 of Solo Founder Marketing

**20% of activities that generate 80% of results:**
1. Direct conversations with potential customers
2. Publishing content targeting real search demand
3. Building and nurturing your email list
4. Building genuine relationships in communities
5. Asking happy customers for referrals

**80% of activities that generate 20% of results (or less):**
1. Tweaking website design
2. A/B testing headline variants (at small scale)
3. Creating social media graphics
4. Researching competitors endlessly
5. Setting up complex marketing automation
6. Attending networking events without follow-up
7. Reading about marketing instead of doing it
