## The Core Principle

**Traditional SEO optimizes for rankings. GEO optimizes for citations.** When a user asks an AI assistant a question, the model synthesizes an answer from multiple sources and cites the ones it trusts most. Your job is to become one of those cited sources.

The shift is fundamental: you are no longer competing for ten blue links. You are competing for inclusion in a single, synthesized answer. If you are not cited, you are invisible. There is no "page two" of an AI response.

GEO is to AI search what SEO is to traditional search. They are complementary, not competing strategies.

Three forces determine whether your content gets cited by AI engines:

1. **Structure** — Make your content extractable (easy for a model to pull a clean answer from).
2. **Authority** — Make your content citable (signals that tell the model this source is trustworthy).
3. **Presence** — Be where AI looks (your own site, but also third-party sources the model trusts).

Every section of this playbook maps to one or more of these pillars.

## Part 1: The Three Pillars of AI Visibility

### How AI Search Differs from Traditional Search

| Dimension | Traditional Search (Google) | AI Search (ChatGPT, Perplexity) |
|-----------|--------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Output** | List of links | Direct answers with citations |
| **User behavior** | Click through to websites | Read answer, may click citations |
| **Ranking signal** | Backlinks, on-page SEO, site authority | Content quality, specificity, authority, recency |
| **Content format** | Keyword-optimized pages | Comprehensive, well-structured, citable content |
| **Traffic pattern** | Consistent organic traffic | Variable, citation-based referrals |
| **Competition** | Other websites | Being cited as a source vs. being summarized without credit |

### How LLMs Select and Cite Sources

LLMs prefer to cite content that has these properties:

| Citation Factor | What It Means | How to Optimize |
|----------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Authority** | Recognized entity or expert source | Build topical authority, earn quality backlinks |
| **Specificity** | Concrete data, numbers, frameworks | Include original statistics, specific examples |
| **Recency** | Up-to-date information | Update content regularly, include dates |
| **Structure** | Clear organization, headings, lists | Use H2/H3 headings, tables, numbered lists |
| **Uniqueness** | Original research or perspective | Create original data, proprietary frameworks |
| **Corroboration** | Consistent with other trusted sources | Cite your own sources, align with established facts |

## Part 3: GEO Content Strategy

### 8 Core GEO Tactics

#### 1. Create Definitive Content
Be THE authoritative source on specific topics. LLMs prefer comprehensive, authoritative pieces.

**Format:** "The Complete Guide to [Topic]" or "Everything You Need to Know About [Topic]"

**Requirements:**
- Cover the topic more thoroughly than any other source
- Include original insights, not just aggregated information
- Update at least quarterly with new data or perspectives
- Structure with clear headings that match common questions

#### 2. Publish Original Data and Statistics
AI models heavily cite statistics and data points. Creating your own data is the fastest path to AI citation.

| Data Type | Creation Method | Citation Potential |
|-----------|----------------|:-----------------:|
| **Customer surveys** | Survey 200+ users, publish findings | Very High |
| **Internal analytics** | Analyze your own product/service data | Very High |
| **Industry benchmarks** | Aggregate and normalize public data | High |
| **Expert polls** | Survey 50+ industry experts | High |
| **Case study metrics** | Document client/customer results | Medium-High |

**Formula:** "According to [Your Brand]'s [Year] [Study Name], [X]% of [audience] [finding]."

#### 3. Write Direct Answer Content
Structure content to directly answer questions that people ask AI:

```
## What is [Topic]?

[Topic] is [clear, concise definition in 1-2 sentences].

[2-3 sentences of additional context]

### Key characteristics of [Topic]:
1. [Characteristic 1]
2. [Characteristic 2]
3. [Characteristic 3]
```

**Target questions:** Use tools like AlsoAsked, AnswerThePublic, and "People Also Ask" to identify questions your audience asks.

#### 4. Build Comparison and "Best Of" Content
AI frequently cites comparison content when users ask "What's the best [X]?"

**Structure:**
- Clear comparison criteria
- Objective scoring methodology
- Pros/cons for each option
- Recommendation by use case
- Regular updates (quarterly minimum)

#### 5. Create Glossaries and Definitions
AI models frequently pull from well-structured definition pages.

**Format:**
```
## [Term]

**Definition:** [Clear, authoritative 1-sentence definition]

**In context:** [How this term is used in practice]

**Related terms:** [2-3 related concepts with brief definitions]

**Example:** [Concrete example of the term in action]
```

#### 6. Develop Proprietary Frameworks
Named frameworks get cited by AI because they are unique, attributable, and memorable.

**Framework creation checklist:**
- [ ] Does it have a memorable name?
- [ ] Can it be described in one sentence?
- [ ] Does it include a visual (matrix, pyramid, flowchart)?
- [ ] Is it backed by data or experience?
- [ ] Is it useful enough that others will reference it?

#### 7. Publish How-To Guides with Steps
Step-by-step guides are frequently cited by AI for actionable queries.

**Structure:**
```
## How to [Achieve Outcome] in [Timeframe]

### Step 1: [Action Verb] [Object]
[Detailed instructions]
[Example or screenshot]

### Step 2: [Action Verb] [Object]
[Detailed instructions]
[Example or screenshot]

[Continue for all steps]

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. [Mistake + why it fails + what to do instead]
```

#### 8. Structure Content for Machine Readability

| Element | Implementation | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| **Clear H2/H3 hierarchy** | One H1, logical H2/H3 nesting | AI parses document structure |
| **Tables for comparisons** | Use HTML/markdown tables for any comparative data | Tables are easy for AI to extract |
| **Numbered/bulleted lists** | Use for any sequential or grouped information | Lists are highly citable |
| **Bold key terms** | Bold important definitions and key phrases | Helps AI identify core concepts |
| **Internal linking** | Link to your own related content | Builds topical authority |
| **Schema markup** | Article, FAQ, HowTo, Product schema | Structured data for AI parsers |

## Part 4: Princeton GEO Research Data (KDD 2024)

The most rigorous study on Generative Engine Optimization to date. Aggarwal et al. at Princeton tested specific content optimization strategies against a Perplexity.ai-style generative engine and measured visibility changes. Here are the full findings.

### Individual Strategy Impact

| Strategy | Visibility Change | Description |
|----------|:-----------------:|-------------|
| Citing sources | **+40%** | Adding references to authoritative external sources |
| Adding statistics | **+37%** | Including specific numbers, percentages, data points |
| Including quotations | **+30%** | Expert quotes that lend credibility |
| Authoritative tone | **+25%** | Confident, expert voice (not hedging or uncertain) |
| Improving clarity | **+20%** | Clearer sentence structure, better organization |
| Technical terms | **+18%** | Domain-specific vocabulary that signals expertise |
| Fluency optimization | **+15-30%** | Natural, well-written prose (varies by baseline quality) |
| Keyword stuffing | **-10%** | Unnatural keyword repetition actively hurts visibility |

### Combination Effects

Not all strategies combine linearly. The Princeton research found specific combinations that amplify each other:

| Combination | Effect | Why It Works |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| Fluency + Statistics | **Maximum boost** | Natural prose with hard data is the most authoritative and extractable format |
| Sources + Quotations | Very strong | External validation from multiple signal types |
| Clarity + Technical terms | Strong | Accessible expertise — clear but precise |
| Statistics + Authoritative tone | Strong | Confident claims backed by data |
| Keyword stuffing + Anything | Negative | Keyword stuffing degrades the effectiveness of every other strategy |

### Impact by Current Ranking

The most striking finding: **low-ranking sites benefit the most from GEO optimization.**

| Current Ranking | Potential Visibility Increase | Implication |
|:--------------:|:----------------------------:|-------------|
| Low-ranked (positions 50+) | Up to **115%** | GEO is the great equalizer — unknown sites can leapfrog established ones |
| Mid-ranked (positions 10-50) | 50-80% | Strong returns; GEO moves you into citation range |
| High-ranked (positions 1-10) | 10-30% | Already visible; GEO protects and incrementally improves position |

**Key insight:** If you are a small or new brand, GEO is your highest-leverage SEO investment. Traditional SEO rewards incumbents. GEO rewards quality and structure regardless of domain authority.

## Part 5: Content Extractability

AI models do not "read" your page the way a human does. They scan for discrete, self-contained answer blocks they can pull into a synthesized response. Your content must be structured for extraction.

### The Self-Contained Answer Block

Every key concept on your page should have a passage that answers a question completely without requiring surrounding context. Think of each block as a standalone unit that could appear in an AI response and make perfect sense.

**Bad (requires context):**
```
As we mentioned above, this approach works well because of the factors
we discussed in section 2. The results speak for themselves.
```

**Good (self-contained):**
```
Content pruning — the practice of removing or consolidating low-performing
pages — typically improves overall site authority by 15-25%. Sites with
fewer than 500 pages see the strongest gains because search engines can
allocate more crawl budget to remaining high-quality pages.
```

### Optimal Answer Passage Length

| Passage Length | Use Case | Extraction Likelihood |
|:-------------:|----------|:--------------------:|
| 20-30 words | Definitions, quick facts | High for simple queries |
| 40-60 words | Explanations, how-it-works | Highest overall |
| 60-80 words | Detailed answers, nuanced topics | High for complex queries |
| 80-120 words | Comprehensive overviews | Medium (may be truncated) |
| 120+ words | Too long for extraction | Low — will be paraphrased |

**Target:** 40-60 words per key answer passage. This is the sweet spot for AI snippet extraction.

### Heading Strategy

Your H2 and H3 headings should match the way people phrase questions to AI. AI engines map user queries to page headings to locate relevant content.

| Instead of This Heading | Use This Heading |
|------------------------|-----------------|
| "Overview" | "What Is [Topic] and How Does It Work?" |
| "Benefits" | "Why [Topic] Matters for [Audience]" |
| "Process" | "How to [Do Thing] in [N] Steps" |
| "Pricing" | "How Much Does [Product] Cost in 2025?" |
| "Comparison" | "[Product A] vs [Product B]: Key Differences" |
| "FAQ" | Keep "FAQ" but make each question a real H3 |

### Format Hierarchy for AI Extraction

Some formats are dramatically easier for AI to extract and cite than others:

| Rank | Format | Why AI Prefers It |
|:----:|--------|------------------|
| 1 | Tables | Structured data with clear relationships; easy to parse and cite |
| 2 | Numbered lists | Sequential information with clear ordering |
| 3 | Bullet lists | Discrete facts, easy to pull individual items |
| 4 | Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences) | Self-contained, quotable |
| 5 | Long paragraphs (5+ sentences) | Harder to extract; often paraphrased instead of cited |

**Rule of thumb:** Tables beat prose for comparisons. Numbered lists beat paragraphs for processes. Bullets beat paragraphs for features and benefits.

## Part 6: Entity Optimization

### Making Your Brand a Recognized AI Entity

AI models need to "know" your brand as a distinct entity to recommend it.

**5 Entity Building Strategies:**

1. **Consistent naming:** Always refer to your brand, product, and frameworks with the exact same name across all content
2. **Wikipedia-style presence:** Create or contribute to relevant Wikipedia articles, Wikidata entries (the legitimate way — through notable coverage)
3. **Cross-reference network:** Get mentioned in other authoritative sources (press coverage, industry reports, partner content)
4. **Structured data:** Implement Organization, Product, and Person schema markup on your website
5. **Knowledge panel optimization:** Ensure your Google Knowledge Panel is accurate and complete

### Topical Authority Building

To become THE cited source for a topic:

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|------|--------|:--------:|
| 1 | Map all subtopics in your domain | Week 1 |
| 2 | Create pillar content for top 5 subtopics | Month 1-2 |
| 3 | Publish cluster content (5-10 pieces per pillar) | Month 2-4 |
| 4 | Build internal linking between all related content | Ongoing |
| 5 | Earn external citations and backlinks | Ongoing |
| 6 | Update and expand content quarterly | Quarterly |

**Topical authority formula:** Breadth (number of subtopics covered) x Depth (comprehensiveness of each piece) x Freshness (recency of updates) x Citations (external references to your content)

## Part 7: Third-Party Presence Strategy

This is the most underappreciated finding in AI SEO: **brands are 6.5x more likely to be cited through third-party sources than through their own domains.**

AI models cross-reference multiple sources. When your brand appears on trusted third-party sites, those sites get cited — and your brand gets mentioned inside that citation. Controlling your third-party presence is not optional.

### Top Third-Party Citation Sources

| Source | Share of Citations (ChatGPT) | Priority | Action |
|--------|:---------------------------:|:--------:|--------|
| Wikipedia | 7.8% | Critical | Create/improve your Wikipedia page; ensure accuracy |
| Reddit | 1.8% | High | Participate authentically; create useful posts in relevant subreddits |
| G2 / Capterra / TrustRadius | High for SaaS | High | Actively solicit reviews; respond to all reviews |
| YouTube | Frequently cited by Google AI Overviews | High | Create video content; optimize titles and descriptions |
| Industry blogs / publications | Varies by niche | Medium-High | Guest post; get featured; provide expert quotes |
| Stack Overflow / GitHub | High for developer tools | Conditional | Answer questions; maintain active repos |
| Quora | Moderate | Medium | Answer relevant questions with genuine expertise |
| News / press coverage | Varies | Medium | PR efforts; newsworthy announcements |

### Third-Party Optimization Playbook

**Wikipedia:**
- Do NOT edit your own Wikipedia page (violation of Wikipedia policy and easily detected)
- Ensure your brand has enough "notability" (press coverage, third-party references)
- Provide reliable sources that Wikipedia editors can use
- Monitor your page for inaccuracies using WikiAlerts or similar tools
- Keep your Wikidata entry accurate and complete (AI models pull from Wikidata)

**Reddit:**
- Build genuine karma over time — do not create shill accounts
- Answer questions in your niche subreddits with real expertise
- Create "definitive guide" posts that the community upvotes
- Monitor brand mentions with tools or RSS feeds
- Never overtly promote — Reddit detects and punishes this

**Review Sites (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius):**
- Claim and complete your profile on all major review platforms
- Ask satisfied customers for reviews at key moments (post-onboarding, after a win)
- Respond to every review, positive and negative
- Keep feature lists, pricing, and screenshots up to date
- Aim for 50+ reviews on G2 to appear in their "Grid" reports (heavily cited by AI)

**YouTube:**
- Create videos that answer the same questions your blog posts answer
- Use exact-match query phrasing in video titles ("How to [do thing]")
- Add comprehensive descriptions with timestamps
- Google AI Overviews frequently cite YouTube videos — this is a direct pipeline

## Part 8: Schema Markup for AI

Structured data (schema.org markup) gives AI engines explicit signals about what your content is, who wrote it, and how it is organized. Content with proper schema shows **30-40% higher AI visibility** compared to equivalent content without it.

### Priority Schema Types

| Schema Type | Use On | AI Benefit |
|------------|--------|-----------|
| `Article` / `BlogPosting` | Blog posts, guides, articles | Identifies content type, author, date, publisher |
| `HowTo` | Step-by-step tutorials | AI can extract individual steps as structured answers |
| `FAQPage` | FAQ sections | Each Q&A pair becomes a discrete extractable unit |
| `Product` | Product pages | Price, availability, ratings become structured data |
| `ItemList` | Listicles, rankings, best-of | Ordered items are easily cited in list format |
| `Review` / `AggregateRating` | Review pages | Star ratings and review counts appear in AI summaries |
| `Organization` | About page, homepage | Brand identity, logo, social profiles, founding info |
| `Person` | Author pages, team pages | Author expertise signals (E-E-A-T) |
| `BreadcrumbList` | All pages | Helps AI understand site hierarchy and topic relationships |

### Combining Schema with @graph

Use the `@graph` array to combine multiple schema types on a single page. This is the recommended approach for pages that serve multiple purposes (e.g., a blog post with an FAQ section and author bio).

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@graph": [
    {
      "@type": "Article",
      "headline": "Complete Guide to Content Marketing in 2025",
      "author": {
        "@type": "Person",
        "name": "Jane Smith",
        "url": "https://example.com/about/jane-smith"
      },
      "datePublished": "2025-03-15",
      "dateModified": "2025-06-01",
      "publisher": {
        "@type": "Organization",
        "name": "Example Co",
        "logo": {
          "@type": "ImageObject",
          "url": "https://example.com/logo.png"
        }
      }
    },
    {
      "@type": "FAQPage",
      "mainEntity": [
        {
          "@type": "Question",
          "name": "What is content marketing?",
          "acceptedAnswer": {
            "@type": "Answer",
            "text": "Content marketing is a strategy focused on creating and distributing valuable content to attract and retain a target audience."
          }
        }
      ]
    },
    {
      "@type": "BreadcrumbList",
      "itemListElement": [
        { "@type": "ListItem", "position": 1, "name": "Home", "item": "https://example.com/" },
        { "@type": "ListItem", "position": 2, "name": "Blog", "item": "https://example.com/blog/" },
        { "@type": "ListItem", "position": 3, "name": "Content Marketing Guide" }
      ]
    }
  ]
}
```

### Implementation by Platform

| Platform | How to Add Schema | Notes |
|----------|------------------|-------|
| Static HTML sites | Add `<script type="application/ld+json">` in the `<head>` | Fastest; full control |
| Next.js / React | Use `next/head` or `next-seo` package; render JSON-LD in `<Head>` | Generate dynamically from page data |
| WordPress | Use Yoast SEO, Rank Math, or Schema Pro plugins | Automatic for basic types; customize for advanced |
| Webflow | Use custom code embed in page `<head>` settings | Per-page or site-wide via project settings |
| Shopify | Use JSON-LD for SEO app or add to `theme.liquid` | Product schema is partially built-in |

## Part 10: Content Optimization Checklist

Use this checklist when optimizing existing content for AI visibility. Work through it page by page, starting with your highest-traffic pages.

### Per-Page Optimization Checklist

**Structure (Extractability):**
- [ ] Every key point has a self-contained answer block (40-60 words, no dependencies on surrounding text)
- [ ] H2/H3 headings are phrased as questions or match common query patterns
- [ ] Comparisons use tables, not prose paragraphs
- [ ] Processes use numbered lists, not narrative descriptions
- [ ] Features and benefits use bullet lists
- [ ] Page has a clear, concise definition/summary in the first 100 words
- [ ] Each major section can stand alone if extracted out of context

**Authority (Citability):**
- [ ] Content cites at least 3-5 external authoritative sources with links
- [ ] At least 2-3 specific statistics or data points are included
- [ ] At least 1 expert quotation is included (with attribution)
- [ ] Tone is confident and authoritative, not hedging ("we believe" / "it seems")
- [ ] Domain-specific technical terms are used accurately (not dumbed down to the point of losing precision)
- [ ] Author byline links to a comprehensive author page with credentials

**Presence (Discoverability):**
- [ ] Page has proper schema markup (`Article`, `FAQPage`, `HowTo`, etc.)
- [ ] Content topic is also covered on at least one third-party platform (YouTube, Reddit, review site)
- [ ] Internal links connect this page to related content on your site
- [ ] Page is included in your XML sitemap
- [ ] Page loads in under 3 seconds (AI crawlers have timeout limits)

### Content Refresh Protocol

For existing pages, apply this optimization in priority order:

1. **Add statistics** — Find and insert 3-5 relevant data points with sources
2. **Add source citations** — Link to authoritative references throughout
3. **Restructure for extraction** — Break long paragraphs into tables, lists, and answer blocks
4. **Match headings to queries** — Rewrite H2/H3 tags to reflect how people actually ask questions
5. **Add schema markup** — Implement the appropriate schema type(s)
6. **Add expert quotes** — Include quotations from recognized authorities
7. **Strengthen tone** — Remove hedging language; state findings and recommendations with confidence

## Part 11: SEO + GEO Integration

### Unified Content Strategy

| SEO Focus | GEO Focus | Unified Approach |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Keyword targeting | Question answering | Target keywords AND the questions behind them |
| Backlinks | Authority signals | Build both links and brand mentions |
| On-page optimization | Content structure | Optimize for both crawlers and LLMs |
| Long-tail keywords | Conversational queries | Create content for both search formats |
| Featured snippets | AI citations | Structure for both featured snippets and AI extraction |

### Content Template: SEO + GEO Optimized

```
# [Primary Keyword] — [Compelling Descriptor]

## What Is [Primary Keyword]?
[Clear definition — 2 sentences. This is your GEO-optimized intro.]
```

## Part 12: Monitoring & Measurement

AI SEO is harder to measure than traditional SEO because AI engines do not provide analytics dashboards (yet). Here is how to track your progress.

### Primary Metrics

| Metric | How to Measure | Frequency |
|--------|---------------|:---------:|
| AI citation frequency | Manual audit: run test queries and count citations | Monthly |
| Brand mentions in AI responses | Run 20+ brand and category queries; record mention rate | Monthly |
| Traffic from AI referrals | Check analytics for referrals from `chat.openai.com`, `perplexity.ai`, `bing.com/chat`, etc. | Weekly |
| AI Overview inclusion | Search your target queries on Google; check if you appear in AI Overview | Monthly |
| Third-party review volume | Track review counts on G2, Capterra, TrustRadius | Monthly |
| Wikipedia page views | Use Wikipedia Pageviews Analysis tool for your page | Monthly |
| Citation accuracy | Check if AI attributes correctly to your brand | Monthly |
| Query coverage | % of target queries where you are cited | Monthly |
| Competitor citation gaps | Queries where competitors are cited but you are not | Monthly |
| Content citation rate | % of your content pieces cited by AI | Monthly |
| Branded query volume | Search Console branded search trends | Monthly |
| Answer inclusion rate | % of relevant AI answers that include you | Monthly |

### Referral Source Identification

Set up your analytics to track these AI-specific referral sources:

| Referral Source | AI Engine | What It Means |
|----------------|-----------|--------------|
| `chat.openai.com` | ChatGPT | User clicked a link ChatGPT cited from your site |
| `perplexity.ai` | Perplexity | User clicked through from a Perplexity answer |
| `bing.com/chat` or `copilot.microsoft.com` | Copilot | User clicked from a Copilot response |
| `gemini.google.com` | Gemini | User clicked from a Gemini response |
| Google organic with 0 keyword data | Google AI Overviews | Likely AI Overview click-through (hard to isolate precisely) |

### Tracking Spreadsheet Template

Set up a monthly tracking sheet with these columns:

```
| Date | Query Tested | AI Engine | Cited? (Y/N) | Citation Position | Source Type | Competitor Cited? | Notes |
```

Run 20-30 queries per month. Over time, you will see:
- Which content types get cited most for your brand
- Which AI engines cite you most frequently
- Whether optimization efforts are increasing citation rates
- Which competitors consistently appear and on what topics

### Leading vs. Lagging Indicators

| Leading Indicators (Inputs) | Lagging Indicators (Outcomes) |
|:---------------------------:|:-----------------------------:|

| New external citations/backlinks | Domain authority trend |

Focus your weekly work on leading indicators. Measure lagging indicators monthly to confirm the strategy is working.

## Part 14: Key Takeaways

1. **GEO is SEO's complement, not replacement.** Do both.
2. **Original data is your #1 GEO asset.** AI cites statistics more than opinions.
3. **Structure matters as much as substance.** Tables, lists, and clear headings are AI-friendly.
4. **Build your entity.** AI must recognize your brand as authoritative to recommend it.
5. **Monitor actively.** Ask AI about your category weekly — track if and how you appear.
6. **Update content regularly.** AI prefers fresh, maintained content.
7. **Name your frameworks.** Proprietary, named methodologies get cited because they are uniquely attributable.
8. **Third-party presence is critical.** Brands are 6.5x more likely to be cited through third-party sources than their own domains.
9. **Allow all AI bots.** Blocking crawlers makes you invisible to AI engines entirely.
10. **Low-ranking sites benefit most.** GEO is the great equalizer — unknown sites can see up to 115% visibility increase.

### Content Engine Protocol Addition

When producing any content, the Content Engine should:
1. **Structure every key point as a self-contained answer block** (40-60 words, no context dependencies)
2. **Include at least 3 statistics with sources** per long-form piece
3. **Use tables for all comparisons** and numbered lists for all processes
4. **Match H2/H3 headings to actual query phrasing** from keyword research
5. **Generate schema markup** alongside every published page
6. **Flag pages missing AI optimization** during content audits

### Quality Guardian Checkpoint

Before any content is published, the Quality Guardian should verify:
- [ ] Self-contained answer blocks present for all key points
- [ ] At least 3 cited sources with links
- [ ] At least 2 statistics with attribution
- [ ] Schema markup implemented and validated (use Google Rich Results Test)
- [ ] Headings match query-style phrasing
- [ ] No keyword stuffing (density < 2%)
- [ ] Page accessible to all AI bots (robots.txt verified)
