## The Core Principle

> A community is not a Facebook group. A community is a group of people who share a common identity, help each other succeed, and feel a sense of belonging.

Community is the only marketing channel where your customers do the marketing for you. When done right, it becomes a self-sustaining growth loop: members attract new members, create content, answer questions, and become your most vocal advocates.

**Key insight:** Community-Led Growth (CLG) compounds slower than other motions but creates the deepest moats. Community members become your best content creators, product advisors, and referral sources.

## Part 1: Should You Build a Community?

### What Is Community-Led Growth?

Community-Led Growth (CLG) is a go-to-market strategy where community is the primary driver of acquisition, activation, retention, and expansion. Unlike communities built as a marketing afterthought, CLG puts community at the center of the growth engine.

**CLG vs other growth motions:**

| Motion | Primary Engine | Best Metric | Timeline |
|--------|---------------|-------------|:--------:|
| **Product-Led** | Self-serve product experience | Activation rate | Medium |
| **Sales-Led** | Human sales process | Win rate | Long |
| **Content-Led** | Organic content & SEO | Organic traffic | Long |
| **Community-Led** | Peer-to-peer value exchange | Active member rate | Very Long |
| **Partner-Led** | Ecosystem partnerships | Partner-sourced pipeline | Medium |

### Community vs. Audience

| Audience | Community |
|----------|----------|
| You -> many (broadcast) | Many <-> many (conversation) |
| They consume your content | They create content for each other |
| Metrics: followers, views | Metrics: engagement, retention, contribution |
| You are the value | The network is the value |
| Platform risk (algorithm changes) | Platform stability (owned relationships) |

### The Community Readiness Checklist

| Requirement | Your Status | Notes |
|------------|:----------:|-------|
| You have 100+ customers or email subscribers | Yes / No | Don't start a community with 10 people |
| Your ICP has an identity they're proud of | Yes / No | "Founders," "creators," "marketers" — not "people who use our tool" |
| Members would benefit from connecting with each other | Yes / No | Peer learning, networking, accountability |
| You can commit 5-10 hours/week to community | Yes / No | Neglected communities are worse than no community |
| Your product/service benefits from shared learning | Yes / No | Tips, templates, strategies, workflows |

**Score:** 4-5 Yes = Build it. 2-3 Yes = Wait. 0-1 Yes = Don't.

### Community-Market Fit Assessment

Score each factor 1-5:

| Factor | Question | Score (1-5) |
|--------|----------|:----------:|
| **Practice complexity** | Do users need to learn skills to succeed with your product/market? | |
| **Peer learning value** | Would users benefit from hearing how others use the product? | |
| **Identity connection** | Do users identify with the role/practice your product serves? | |
| **Network effects** | Does the community get better as more people join? | |
| **Content potential** | Can members create valuable content for each other? | |
| **Emotional connection** | Do people feel passionate about this space? | |

**Score interpretation:**
- **25-30:** Strong CLG fit — invest heavily
- **18-24:** Moderate fit — start with community, scale based on engagement
- **12-17:** Weak fit — community as supplement, not primary motion
- **<12:** Not a fit — focus on other growth motions

## Part 4: Platform Selection

### Comparison Matrix

| Platform | Setup | Cost | Async | Real-time | Events | Search | Ownership |
|----------|:-----:|:----:|:-----:|:---------:|:------:|:------:|:---------:|
| **Slack** | Easy | Free-$8/user | Good | Great | Weak | Poor | Low |
| **Discord** | Easy | Free | Good | Great | Medium | Poor | Low |
| **Circle** | Medium | $49-399/mo | Great | Medium | Good | Good | High |
| **Custom (Discourse, etc.)** | Hard | Variable | Great | Weak | Weak | Great | Full |
| **LinkedIn Group** | Easy | Free | Medium | Weak | Weak | Weak | None |

### Platform Selection Decision Tree

```
Is real-time conversation important?
├── YES → Is your audience technical?
│   ├── YES → Discord
│   └── NO  → Slack
└── NO  → Do you need SEO value from community content?
    ├── YES → Custom (Discourse) or Circle
    └── NO  → Circle
```

**Rule:** Don't over-invest in platform. Start with Slack or Discord (where people already are), migrate to custom only when you outgrow it.

## Part 5: Community Architecture

### Channel Structure (Recommended)

| Channel | Purpose | Who Posts |
|---------|---------|----------|
| #welcome | New member intros (template provided) | New members |
| #wins | Celebrate achievements and results | Members |
| #questions | Ask for help, get peer answers | Members |
| #resources | Share tools, templates, articles | Members + admin |
| #feedback | Product/service feedback and feature requests | Members |
| #general | Off-topic, water cooler conversation | Everyone |
| #announcements | Official updates, events, launches | Admin only |
| #showcase | Members share their work for feedback | Members |

### Onboarding Flow for New Members

| Step | When | What | Purpose |
|:----:|------|------|---------|
| 1 | Immediately | Welcome DM with community guide | Orientation |
| 2 | Day 0 | Prompt to introduce themselves in #welcome | First engagement |
| 3 | Day 1 | "Here's how to get the most value" email/DM | Activation |
| 4 | Day 3 | Tag them in a relevant conversation | Connection |
| 5 | Day 7 | Check-in: "How's it going? Need anything?" | Retention |
| 6 | Day 14 | Invite to participate in upcoming event | Deepening |

**Welcome Message Template:**
```
Hey [Name], welcome to [Community]!

Here's how to get the most out of this:

1. Introduce yourself in #welcome — tell us what you're working on
2. Check out #resources for [top resource]
3. Ask your first question in #questions — no question is too basic
4. Join us [day] for [regular event]

If you need anything, DM me directly. Glad you're here.
```

## Part 6: Launch Playbook

### Phase 1: Founding Members (Week 1-4)

| Task | Detail | Target |
|------|--------|:------:|
| Identify founding members | Existing customers, power users, friends, network | 20-50 people |
| Personal invitations | Personalized DMs/emails explaining the vision | 80% acceptance rate |
| Define initial channels/topics | 3-5 channels max (start focused) | — |
| Create "About" and guidelines | Clear purpose, rules, expectations | — |
| Seed initial discussions | Post 2-3 conversation starters per day | — |
| Welcome each member personally | Send DM to every new member | 100% |

**Founding member criteria:**
- Active in the space (not lurkers)
- Willing to contribute (not just consume)
- Representative of your target audience
- Positive and constructive communicators
- Mix of experienced and emerging practitioners

### Phase 2: Soft Launch (Week 5-8)

| Task | Detail | Target |
|------|--------|:------:|
| Open to waitlist/invite-only | Create exclusivity to drive demand | 50-200 members |
| Establish engagement rituals | Weekly thread, AMAs, office hours | 2-3 rituals/week |
| Identify early champions | Members who help others, create content | 5-10 champions |
| First community event | AMA with expert, workshop, or roundtable | 20+ attendees |
| Gather feedback | What's working? What's missing? | Survey or poll |

### Phase 3: Growth (Month 3-6)

| Task | Detail | Target |
|------|--------|:------:|
| Open registration | Remove invite-only (or reduce barriers) | 200-1000 members |
| Launch ambassador program | Formalize champion roles with perks | 10-20 ambassadors |
| Content engine from community | Member spotlights, best-of threads, curated insights | Weekly content |
| Cross-promote with other channels | Social, newsletter, website references | Consistent |
| Track community metrics | Active rate, contribution rate, NPS | Dashboard |

### Phase 4: Scale (Month 6+)

| Task | Detail | Target |
|------|--------|:------:|
| Sub-communities | Create topic-specific or role-specific groups | 3-5 sub-groups |
| Events cadence | Regular events (weekly casual, monthly featured, quarterly big) | Consistent |
| Community-to-product feedback loop | Structured input from community to product team | Monthly |
| Revenue attribution | Track community -> pipeline -> revenue | Measured |
| Hire community manager | Dedicated community team member | When >500 active members |

## Part 7: Engagement System

### The Member Journey

```
JOIN → FIRST VALUE → CONTRIBUTE → LEAD → ADVOCATE
  │         │              │          │        │
  │    Get helped,    Help others, Run events, Refer new
  │    learn from     share         mentor     members,
  │    others         experience    others     create
  │                                            content
  └──────── Each stage deepens commitment ──────┘
```

### Stage-by-Stage Engagement

| Stage | Goal | Tactics | Success Metric |
|-------|------|---------|:-------------:|
| **Join** | Create welcoming first experience | Personal welcome DM, onboarding guide, intro thread | 100% welcomed |
| **First Value** | Deliver value within 48 hours | Answer their first question, invite to relevant discussion, share resource | >50% engage in Week 1 |
| **Contribute** | Convert consumers into contributors | Ask for their opinion, tag in relevant threads, celebrate contributions | >20% contribute monthly |
| **Lead** | Empower members to run activities | Ambassador program, event hosting, content creation | >5% become leaders |
| **Advocate** | Turn leaders into growth engine | Referral rewards, co-creation opportunities, public recognition | >2% actively refer |

### The Weekly Community Calendar

| Day | Activity | Time Investment | Goal |
|-----|---------|:--------------:|------|
| Mon | Discussion prompt / "Monday Question" | 15 min | Spark conversation |
| Tue | Share a resource or framework | 15 min | Value delivery |
| Wed | Member spotlight or win celebration | 15 min | Recognition |
| Thu | AMA, live session, or workshop | 30-60 min | Deep engagement |
| Fri | Weekend challenge or reflection | 15 min | Accountability |

### Engagement Rituals (Pick 3-5)

| Ritual | Frequency | Format | Engagement Level |
|--------|:---------:|--------|:----------------:|
| **Weekly discussion thread** | Weekly | "What's working for you this week?" | Low barrier |
| **AMA with expert** | Bi-weekly | Live Q&A with industry expert or customer | High engagement |
| **Office hours** | Weekly | Open call for questions and advice | Medium |
| **Member spotlight** | Weekly | Interview/profile of a community member | Medium |
| **Challenge/workshop** | Monthly | Hands-on activity with deliverable | Very high |
| **Show & share** | Bi-weekly | Members share their work for feedback | High |
| **Hot take/debate** | Monthly | Controversial topic, structured discussion | Very high |
| **Resource share** | Weekly | Curated best resources of the week | Low barrier |

### Engagement Tactics

| Tactic | Frequency | Impact |
|--------|:---------:|:------:|
| **Discussion prompts** | 3-5x/week | Medium |
| **Member spotlights** | 1x/week | High |
| **Live events/workshops** | 1-2x/month | Very High |
| **Challenges** (7-day, 30-day) | 1x/month | High |
| **Expert AMAs** | 1-2x/month | High |
| **Resource drops** | 2-3x/week | Medium |
| **Personal DMs to active members** | 5-10x/week | Very High |
| **Welcome every new member personally** | Ongoing | High |

### Activating Lurkers

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Joined but never posted | DM: "Hey! What are you working on? Happy to point you to relevant threads." |
| Used to be active, went quiet | DM: "Haven't seen you in a while! Everything going well with [last topic]?" |
| Reads but never comments | Tag them in a relevant discussion: "@[name] I think you'd have a great perspective on this" |
| Only engages in DMs | Encourage public sharing: "This insight is too good for DMs — would you share it in #general?" |

### The Engagement Flywheel

```
Member joins → Gets value → Contributes → Gets recognized
       ↑                                        ↓
       └────────── Invites others ←────────────┘
```

## Part 8: Community Content Pipeline

### How Members Create Your Best Marketing

| Content Type | How to Generate | How to Use |
|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Success stories** | Ask members to share wins in community | Turn into case studies and social proof |
| **Best practices** | Curate top advice threads | Turn into blog posts and guides |
| **Templates/frameworks** | Ask members to share their processes | Build template library |
| **Discussions/debates** | Facilitate thoughtful discussions | Turn into thought leadership content |
| **Product feedback** | Structured feedback requests | Use in product marketing and roadmap |
| **Testimonials** | Ask happy members for quotes | Use on website, ads, email |

### Community-to-Content Pipeline

```
Community Discussion
       ↓
Identify valuable thread/insight
       ↓
Curate and expand into content piece
       ↓
Publish on blog/social (credit the member)
       ↓
Share back to community for feedback
       ↓
Member feels valued → contributes more
       ↓
[Loop]
```

### Content Derivatives from Community

| Community Source | Content Derivative | Channel |
|-----------------|-------------------|---------|
| Great question from a member | Blog post or LinkedIn post answering it | Blog, LinkedIn |

| Expert AMA highlights | Social content series | LinkedIn, X |
| Common questions/mistakes | FAQ page or educational content | Blog, landing page |

## Part 9: Community Events

| Event Type | Frequency | Effort | Impact | Format |
|-----------|:---------:|:------:|:------:|--------|
| **Weekly casual chat** | Weekly | Low | Medium | Open Zoom call, no agenda |
| **AMA with expert** | Bi-weekly | Medium | High | Live Q&A, 45-60 min |

| **Virtual meetup (by geo/role)** | Monthly | Medium | High | Small group, 60 min |
| **Annual conference** | Yearly | Very High | Very High | Full-day or multi-day event |
| **Hackathon/challenge** | Quarterly | High | Very High | Multi-day competitive event |

### Event Playbook

1. **Plan:** Choose topic based on community interest (poll members)
2. **Promote:** 2-week promotion cycle (announcement, reminder, day-of)
3. **Execute:** Show up early, facilitate actively, capture content
4. **Follow up:** Share recording/notes, highlight key insights, thank participants
5. **Measure:** Attendance, engagement during event, post-event feedback score

## Part 10: Community Metrics

### Metrics That Matter

| Metric | Definition | Healthy Target | How to Calculate |
|--------|-----------|:--------------:|-----------------|
| **Active rate** | % of members active in last 30 days | >30% | Monthly active / total members |
| **Contribution rate** | % of actives who post or reply | >20% of actives | Contributors / monthly actives |
| **Response time** | Avg time for questions to get answered | <4 hours | Track question -> first response |
| **NPS** | Member satisfaction (quarterly survey) | >50 | Standard NPS survey |
| **Retention** | % of members active month-over-month | >70% | M2 actives / M1 actives |
| **Growth rate** | New members per month | Steady growth | New members / total members |
| **Event attendance** | % of members attending events | >10% per event | Attendees / total members |
| **Content generation** | Member-created posts per week | Growing | Count weekly |
| **Member churn** | Members lost per month | <5% | Churned / total members |
| **Referrals from members** | Member-sourced new joins | Growing | Count monthly |

### Monthly Community Health Dashboard

| Metric | Target | Actual | Trend |
|--------|:------:|:------:|:-----:|
| **Total members** | | | |
| **Active members** (posted in last 30 days) | >30% of total | | |
| **New members this month** | | | |
| **Member churn this month** | <5% | | |
| **Posts/messages per week** | | | |
| **Avg response time to questions** | <4 hours | | |
| **Events attendance rate** | >20% of members | | |
| **NPS score** (quarterly) | >50 | | |
| **Referrals from members** | | | |
| **Revenue influenced by community** | $ | | |

### The 3 Metrics That Matter Most

1. **DAU/MAU Ratio** — What percentage of monthly active users are active daily? Target: >15%
2. **Week 1 Activation** — What % of new members take a meaningful action in their first week? Target: >50%
3. **Member Retention (90-day)** — What % of members are still active after 90 days? Target: >40%

### Vanity Metrics to Ignore

- Total member count (without active rate context)
- Number of messages (without quality context)
- Number of channels (more isn't better)
- Social media followers of community page

## Part 11: Revenue Attribution

### 4-Step Attribution Process

#### Step 1: Tag Community Members in CRM
Every community member should be tagged in your CRM so you can track their journey:
- Date joined community
- Activity level (active/passive)
- Community engagement score
- Content contributed

#### Step 2: Track Community-Influenced Pipeline
| Attribution Type | How to Track |
|-----------------|-------------|
| **Community-sourced** | First touch was community (joined community -> became lead) |
| **Community-influenced** | Touched community at any point in buying journey |
| **Community-accelerated** | Deals that closed faster because buyer was in community |

#### Step 3: Compare Community vs Non-Community Cohorts

| Metric | Community Members | Non-Community | Difference |
|--------|:-----------------:|:-------------:|:----------:|
| Trial -> Paid conversion | | | |
| Average deal size | | | |
| Sales cycle length | | | |
| Net revenue retention | | | |
| Referral rate | | | |
| Support ticket volume | | | |

#### Step 4: Calculate Community ROI

```
Community ROI = (Community-Attributed Revenue - Community Investment) / Community Investment

Community Investment = Platform cost + Staff cost + Event cost + Content cost
Community Revenue = Community-sourced + Community-influenced (weighted 50%)
```

## Part 12: Scaling Without Losing Intimacy

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|---------|
| Conversations get noisy | Create topic-specific sub-channels |
| New members feel lost | Build onboarding sequence with buddy system |
| Quality drops | Implement quality guidelines, empower moderators |
| Founders can't attend everything | Delegate to community ambassadors and hired community managers |
| Signal gets lost in noise | Create "best of" curation and highlights |
| Engagement plateaus | Introduce new formats, rituals, and challenges regularly |

### The "Dunbar Number" Problem

Communities feel intimate at <150 active members. Beyond that:
- Break into cohorts or sub-groups (geography, role, topic)
- Create "inner circle" for most engaged members
- Use events to create personal connections at scale
- Introduce members to each other through pairing/buddy programs

### The Community Growth Loop

```
Member gets value → Shares publicly → New person discovers → Joins community → Gets value
```

### Growth Tactics by Stage

| Stage | Member Count | Focus | Tactics |
|-------|:-----------:|-------|---------|
| **Seed** | 0-50 | Quality over quantity | Personally invite 50 ideal members, be the most active person |
| **Core** | 50-200 | Build culture and norms | Develop rituals, identify super-members, delegate moderation |
| **Growth** | 200-1K | Sustainable engagement | Launch events, create content from community, member referral program |
| **Scale** | 1K+ | Self-sustaining | Member-led events, sub-communities, ambassador program |

### Member Referral Program

```
Every member gets: [X] invites to share with colleagues/peers

When someone they invite joins:
- Inviter gets: [recognition / exclusive access / credit]
- New member gets: [welcome package / featured intro]
```

## Part 13: Moderation & Governance

### Community Guidelines (Template)

```
Welcome to [Community Name]!

This community exists to [purpose statement].

OUR EXPECTATIONS:
- Be helpful — share knowledge generously
- Be respectful — disagree on ideas, not people
- Be genuine — no spam, no pitches without asking
- Be curious — ask questions, explore perspectives
- Give credit — attribute ideas and resources

NOT ALLOWED:
- Self-promotion without context or value
- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks
- Sharing confidential or proprietary information
- Spam, affiliate links, or MLM pitches
- Off-topic content in focused channels
```

### Moderation Response Framework

| Tier | Behavior | Response | Actor |
|:----:|----------|----------|:-----:|
| 1 | Off-topic post, minor guideline miss | Gentle redirect via DM | Moderator |
| 2 | Self-promotion, low-quality spam | Remove + DM warning | Moderator |
| 3 | Repeated violations after warning | Temporary mute (7 days) | Admin |
| 4 | Harassment, discrimination | Immediate removal | Admin |
| 5 | Illegal activity, doxxing | Immediate ban + report | Admin |

## Part 14: Community Team Hiring

### When to Hire

| Community Size | Team Need |
|:-------------:|-----------|
| 0-200 members | Founder/marketing lead runs it (10 hrs/week) |
| 200-500 members | Part-time community manager or contractor |
| 500-2,000 members | Full-time community manager |
| 2,000-10,000 members | Community manager + community content creator |
| 10,000+ members | Community team (manager, content, events, developer advocate) |

### Community Manager Job Description (Core)

- Design and execute community engagement strategy
- Welcome and onboard new members
- Facilitate discussions and moderate content
- Plan and run community events
- Identify and nurture community champions
- Track metrics and report on community health
- Connect community insights to product and marketing teams
- Create content from community discussions

## Key Takeaways

1. **Community is a long game.** Expect 6-12 months before meaningful ROI. The compounding is worth it.
2. **Start with value, not your product.** Build a community of practice, not a user forum.
3. **Quality > quantity.** 200 engaged members > 5,000 silent ones.
4. **Rituals create habits.** Consistent weekly/monthly rituals drive sustained engagement.
5. **Members are your content team.** The best community content comes from members, not you.
6. **Measure what matters.** Active rate and contribution rate, not total members.
7. **Track revenue impact.** If you can't attribute community to pipeline, it's a hobby, not a strategy.
8. **Hire before you burn out.** Community management is exhausting — hire help at 200+ active members.
