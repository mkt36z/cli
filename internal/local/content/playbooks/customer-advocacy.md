## Part 1: What Customer Advocacy Is (and Isn't)

### Advocacy vs. Loyalty vs. Satisfaction

| Dimension | Satisfied Customer | Loyal Customer | Advocate |
|-----------|------------------|---------------|---------|
| **Behavior** | Uses the product | Renews / repurchases | Actively promotes to others |
| **NPS** | 7-8 (Passive) | 8-9 | 9-10 (Promoter) |
| **Revenue impact** | Maintains revenue | Maintains + expands | Drives new revenue through referrals |
| **Marketing value** | Low | Medium | Very high (authentic proof) |
| **Effort to maintain** | Support | Relationship | Recognition + community |

## Part 2: Building the Advocacy Program

### Step 1: Identify Potential Advocates

| Signal | Where to Find It | Score |
|--------|-----------------|:-----:|
| NPS score 9-10 | NPS surveys | +20 |
| Unsolicited positive feedback | Support tickets, social mentions | +15 |
| Referred another customer | Referral tracking | +25 |
| Expanded / upgraded | CRM | +10 |
| Active in community | Community platform | +10 |
| Published content about you | Social / blog monitoring | +20 |
| Long tenure (12+ months) | CRM | +5 |
| Responded to case study request | Email | +15 |

**Advocacy score >40:** Invite to advocacy program.

### Step 2: Design the Advocacy Tiers

| Tier | Criteria | Asks | Gives Back |
|------|---------|------|-----------|
| **Community Member** | Any happy customer | Social follow, newsletter signup | Early access to content |
| **Contributor** | NPS 9+, 6+ months customer | Review on G2/Capterra, social post | Exclusive community access, swag |
| **Champion** | Active contributor, referral | Case study, speaking opportunity, reference call | Product input, advisory board access, premium features |
| **Ambassador** | Top 10 advocates | Conference speaking, co-content, joint PR | Co-marketing budget, executive access, partnership |

### Step 3: The Ask Ladder

Start small, escalate over time. Never ask a customer for a video testimonial before they've done a G2 review.

| Level | Ask | Effort (Customer) | Value (You) | Typical Timing |
|:-----:|-----|:------------------:|:-----------:|:--------------:|
| 1 | Leave a review (G2, Capterra, etc.) | Low | High (SEO + proof) | Month 3+ |
| 2 | Social media post about their experience | Low | Medium (social proof) | Month 3+ |
| 3 | Provide a quote / testimonial | Low | High (sales collateral) | Month 6+ |
| 4 | Participate in a case study interview | Medium | Very High (deep proof) | Month 6+ |
| 5 | Serve as a reference for prospects | Medium | Very High (close deals) | Month 9+ |
| 6 | Speak at your event or webinar | High | Very High (authority) | Month 12+ |
| 7 | Co-create content (blog post, research) | High | Very High (credibility) | Month 12+ |
| 8 | Joint press release or PR | High | Very High (media value) | Month 12+ |

## Part 3: Running the Program

### Monthly Advocacy Activities

| Activity | Owner | Frequency | Goal |
|----------|-------|:---------:|------|
| Review monitoring (G2, Capterra, social) | Advocacy Manager | Weekly | Track organic advocacy |
| Advocate outreach (asks) | Advocacy Manager | Weekly | 5-10 new asks/month |
| Thank-you and recognition | Advocacy Manager | Weekly | Every advocate gets thanked |
| Content from advocates | Content Team | Monthly | 2-4 advocate-sourced pieces |
| Reference call coordination | Sales + Advocacy | As needed | Support active deals |
| Advisory board meeting | Product + Advocacy | Quarterly | Product input + relationship |

### Advocate Communication Cadence

| Touchpoint | Frequency | Content |
|-----------|:---------:|---------|
| Advocate newsletter | Monthly | Product updates, upcoming opportunities, recognition |
| Personal check-in | Quarterly | Relationship building, satisfaction pulse |
| Advocate event (virtual or in-person) | Bi-annually | Community building, exclusive insights |
| Birthday / anniversary recognition | Annual | Personal touch, show you care |
| Thank-you gift | After each major contribution | Thoughtful (not generic swag) |

### Advocacy ROI Calculation

```
Advocacy Program ROI = (Revenue influenced by advocacy - Program cost) / Program cost

Revenue influenced includes:
  + Referral revenue from advocates
  + Deals closed with reference calls
  + Revenue influenced by case studies in sales process
  + Pipeline generated from advocate-driven events

Program cost includes:
  + Headcount (partial FTE)
  + Gifts and recognition
  + Events
  + Tools
```

## Part 6: Common Advocacy Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Only asking for help, never giving back | Create a genuine value exchange |
| Asking for too much too soon | Follow the ask ladder |
| Treating advocates as a marketing channel | They're partners, not assets |
| No recognition or thank-you | Every contribution deserves acknowledgment |
| Letting the program go stale | Monthly outreach, quarterly events minimum |
| Not sharing results with advocates | Show them the impact their advocacy had |
