## Part 1: The Agency Pricing Spectrum

> **"Price is a creative act. It communicates your confidence, your value, and your positioning."** — Blair Enns

### The 6 Pricing Models

| Model | How It Works | Best For | Risk Level |
|-------|-------------|---------|:---:|
| **Hourly billing** | Time × rate = fee | Compliance, ad hoc work | Low (for agency) |
| **Project-based** | Fixed fee per project | Defined-scope projects | Medium |
| **Retainer** | Fixed monthly fee | Ongoing relationships | Low |
| **Value-based** | Fee based on outcomes/impact | High-value, measurable work | Medium-High |
| **Performance-based** | Fee tied to results | Demand gen, paid media | High |
| **Hybrid** | Base retainer + performance bonus | Growth partnerships | Medium |

## Part 2: Model Deep Dives

### Hourly Billing

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **When to use** | Ad hoc requests, overflow work, compliance-heavy projects |
| **How to set rates** | (Salary × 3) / billable hours = minimum viable rate |
| **Pros** | Low risk, easy to scope, transparent |
| **Cons** | Punishes efficiency, income capped by hours, commoditizes expertise |
| **Best practice** | Use for small projects only. Move to retainer or value-based as soon as possible. |

**Rate benchmarks (US market):**

| Role | Junior | Mid | Senior | Director |
|------|:------:|:---:|:------:|:--------:|
| Strategy | $100-150 | $150-250 | $250-400 | $400-600 |
| Content | $75-125 | $125-200 | $200-350 | $300-500 |
| Design | $75-125 | $125-200 | $200-350 | $300-500 |
| Paid media | $100-150 | $150-250 | $250-400 | $350-500 |
| Development | $100-175 | $175-275 | $275-450 | $400-600 |

### Retainer Model

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **When to use** | Ongoing marketing partnerships, multi-channel execution |
| **How to price** | Estimate monthly hours × blended rate, then add margin |
| **Structure** | Fixed monthly fee for a defined set of deliverables |
| **Pros** | Predictable revenue, deeper client relationships |
| **Cons** | Scope creep risk, can undervalue expertise if not reviewed |

**Retainer pricing tiers:**

| Tier | Monthly Fee | Typical Scope |
|------|:----------:|-------------|
| **Starter** | $3,000-5,000 | 1-2 channels, basic content, monthly reporting |
| **Growth** | $5,000-10,000 | 3-4 channels, full content calendar, bi-weekly strategy |
| **Scale** | $10,000-25,000 | Full-service, multi-channel, weekly strategy, dedicated team |
| **Enterprise** | $25,000+ | Strategic partnership, embedded team, custom everything |

### Value-Based Pricing

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **When to use** | Projects with measurable business impact (launches, repositioning, revenue programs) |
| **How to price** | Quantify the value → charge 10-20% of expected impact |
| **Pros** | Highest margins, aligns incentives, positions you as strategic partner |
| **Cons** | Harder to scope, requires confidence, some clients resist |

**Value-based pricing example:**

```
Client Problem:  Losing $500K/year to competitor. No differentiated positioning.
Expected Value:  Repositioning + messaging → recover $200K in year 1 (conservative)
Your Fee:        $20,000-40,000 (10-20% of value created)
vs. Hourly:      80 hours × $200/hr = $16,000 (leaving $24,000 on the table)
```

### Performance-Based Pricing

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **When to use** | Paid media management, lead generation, revenue-driving campaigns |
| **Structure** | Base fee + performance bonus (or % of ad spend + bonus) |
| **Pros** | Aligns incentives, easy to justify costs |
| **Cons** | Revenue depends on client's product/sales, attribution disputes |

**Common performance structures:**

| Structure | How It Works | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **% of ad spend** | Management fee as % of media budget | 15-20% of spend ($5K spend = $750-1,000/mo) |
| **Base + bonus** | Retainer + bonus for exceeding targets | $3K/mo retainer + $500 per MQL above 100 |
| **Revenue share** | % of revenue generated | 5-15% of attributed revenue |
| **Pay per lead** | Fixed fee per qualified lead | $50-500 per lead (depends on industry) |

## Part 3: Pricing Your Agency

### Step 1: Know Your Numbers

| Metric | Your Number | Healthy Range |
|--------|:-----------:|:------------:|
| **Fully-loaded cost per employee** | $ | — |
| **Target utilization rate** | % | 65-75% |
| **Target profit margin** | % | 20-30% |
| **Blended hourly cost** | $ | (Total costs / Total billable hours) |
| **Effective hourly rate (EHR)** | $ | (Revenue / Total hours worked) |
| **Average revenue per client** | $/mo | — |
| **Client retention rate** | % | >85% |

### Step 2: Calculate Your Minimum Fee

```
Minimum Monthly Fee = (Monthly Overhead + Target Profit) / Number of Clients

Example:
  Monthly overhead: $40,000
  Target profit (25%): $10,000
  Number of clients: 10
  Minimum fee per client: $5,000/month
```

### Step 3: The Value Ladder

| Tier | Price Range | Includes | Profit Margin |
|------|:----------:|---------|:-------------:|
| **One-time project** | $2K-15K | Defined deliverables, fixed scope | 30-50% |
| **Monthly retainer** | $3K-10K | Ongoing execution + strategy | 20-35% |
| **Strategic partnership** | $10K-25K | Full-service, embedded, strategic | 25-40% |
| **Advisory / fractional CMO** | $5K-15K | Strategy only, high-touch | 50-70% |
| **Training / workshop** | $5K-25K | One-time knowledge transfer | 60-80% |

## Part 4: Pricing Conversations

### The Pricing Conversation Framework

| Step | What to Say | Why |
|------|-----------|-----|
| 1. Understand value | "What's this worth to your business if we solve it?" | Anchors price to value, not cost |
| 2. Present options | "We have three approaches at different investment levels" | Avoids take-it-or-leave-it |
| 3. Recommend one | "Based on your goals, I'd recommend [option]" | Shows confidence and advisory positioning |
| 4. Justify the price | "Here's why it's priced this way..." | Connect to outcomes, not hours |
| 5. Handle objections | "What's your biggest concern about moving forward?" | Surface and address objections directly |

### Common Pricing Objections

| Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "That's more than we budgeted" | "What budget did you have in mind? Let me show you what we can do within that range." |
| "Can you break it down by hours?" | "We price based on the value we deliver, not hours spent. Here's the expected outcome..." |
| "[Competitor] quoted less" | "You might be comparing different scopes. Let me show you exactly what's included and the results we guarantee." |
| "Can we start smaller?" | "Absolutely. Here's our starter tier. Many clients begin here and expand." |
| "We'll do some in-house" | "Great. We can complement your team with [specific gap]. Here's what that looks like." |

## Part 5: Pricing Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| **Underpricing to win deals** | Attracts bad clients, unsustainable margins | Price reflects quality. If you lose on price, they weren't your client. |
| **Not raising prices annually** | Costs increase, margins erode | 5-10% annual increase, grandfathered for existing clients |
| **No minimum engagement** | Small clients consume disproportionate time | Set a minimum monthly fee |
| **Scope creep without renegotiation** | Free work erodes profitability | Formal change order process for every out-of-scope request |
| **Discounting without removing scope** | Trains clients to negotiate | If they want a lower price, remove deliverables |
| **Pricing based on cost, not value** | Leaves money on the table | Always start the pricing conversation with value |

## Part 6: Agency Financial Health Dashboard

| Metric | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|--------|:-------:|:-------:|:--------:|
| **Gross margin** | >50% | 40-50% | <40% |
| **Net profit margin** | >20% | 10-20% | <10% |
| **Revenue per employee** | >$150K | $100-150K | <$100K |
| **Client concentration** | No client >20% of revenue | >25% | >40% |
| **Utilization rate** | 65-75% | 50-65% | <50% |
| **Average client lifetime** | >12 months | 6-12 months | <6 months |
| **Revenue growth (YoY)** | >20% | 5-20% | <5% or declining |
