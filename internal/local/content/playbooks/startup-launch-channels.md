# Playbook: Startup Launch Channels — The Complete Distribution Directory

## Why This Playbook Exists
"If you build it, they will come" is a lie. Distribution is the #1 challenge for solo founders. This playbook is the definitive guide to every channel where you can launch, promote, and grow your startup — from $0 platforms to paid channels. Organized by channel type, with expected impact, effort, and tactical playbooks for each.

*Sources: PlacesToPostYourStartup (6.5K stars), Marketing-for-Founders, promote-your-next-startup, Product Hunt community data, and Marketing36z OS analysis of 100+ successful indie launches.*

## Part 2: Launch Platform Deep Dives

### Product Hunt Launch Playbook

**Pre-Launch Checklist (Start 4 weeks before):**

| Week | Action | Purpose |
|:----:|--------|---------|
| -4 | Research top products in your category (engagement patterns, maker comments) | Understand what wins |
| -3 | Build a "coming soon" page collecting emails | Pre-launch audience |
| -3 | Engage daily on Product Hunt (upvote, comment on 5+ products) | Build community presence |
| -2 | Prepare assets: 6+ screenshots, demo GIF/video (60s), logo (240×240) | Visual impact |
| -2 | Write your tagline (60 chars max), description, and maker comment | The real pitch |
| -1 | Line up 10-15 people to write authentic comments on launch day | Social proof wave |
| -1 | Prepare launch announcement for email list, social, and community | Cross-promotion |

**Launch Day Protocol:**

| Time (PT) | Action |
|:---------:|--------|
| 12:01 AM | Go live (earliest possible for maximum front-page time) |
| 6:00 AM | Share on all social channels, email list, communities |
| 8:00 AM | Begin responding to every comment within 30 minutes |
| 10:00 AM | Share progress update on social ("We're #X on Product Hunt!") |
| 12:00 PM | Second social push + direct messages to supporters |
| 3:00 PM | Share specific comment or feedback (screenshot) |
| 6:00 PM | Thank everyone, share preliminary numbers |
| 11:59 PM | Voting period ends |

**Key rules:**
- Never ask people to "upvote" — PH flags this. Say "check out our launch" or "we'd love your feedback"
- The **maker comment** is your real pitch — write it like a sales letter (problem → solution → why now → proof)
- Respond to EVERY comment personally, substantively
- Have real users leave genuine comments about their experience

**Expected results by tier:**
| Finish Position | Votes | Visitors | Signups (est.) |
|:---------------:|:-----:|:--------:|:--------------:|
| #1 of the Day | 500-1500+ | 10K-50K | 500-5,000 |
| #2-#5 | 200-500 | 3K-15K | 200-1,500 |
| #6-#10 | 100-200 | 1K-5K | 100-500 |
| #11-#20 | 50-100 | 500-2K | 50-200 |

### Hacker News (Show HN) Playbook

**Title format:** `Show HN: [Product Name] – [One-line description of what it does]`

**Posting best practices:**
| Factor | Recommendation | Why |
|--------|---------------|-----|
| Time | 8-10 AM ET, Tuesday-Thursday | Peak HN traffic |
| Title | Descriptive, technical, honest | HN penalizes marketing-speak |
| First comment | Detailed: what you built, why, how, honest limitations | HN rewards transparency |
| Engagement | Reply to every comment, be honest about weaknesses | Community values authenticity |
| Technical depth | Share architecture, decisions, trade-offs | Technical audience appreciates depth |

**What works on HN:**
- Open-source projects
- Novel technical approaches
- Solo founder stories (genuine, not bragging)
- Tools that solve real developer/founder problems
- Transparent build-in-public narratives

**What doesn't work on HN:**
- Marketing-speak or hype
- "We're disrupting X" language
- Asking for upvotes (instant penalty)
- Products that don't solve a clear problem
- AI wrappers without genuine differentiation

### Reddit Launch Strategy

**The 90/10 Rule (Critical):**
- 90% of your Reddit presence = genuinely helpful comments, no product mention
- 10% = product mention ONLY when directly relevant to someone's question

**Subreddit launch approach (per subreddit):**

| Phase | Timeline | Activities |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Lurk** | Weeks 1-2 | Read posts, understand culture, note recurring questions |
| **Contribute** | Weeks 3-6 | Answer questions, share expertise, build karma |
| **Earn trust** | Weeks 7-12 | Become recognized as helpful; people start checking your profile |
| **Soft launch** | Week 13+ | Share your product when genuinely relevant, with full disclosure |

**Reddit post types that work:**
| Type | Template | Subreddit |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Milestone post** | "I just hit [milestone] with my side project. Here's what I learned:" | r/sideproject, r/startups |
| **How I built it** | "I built [thing] with [tech]. Here's the architecture:" | r/webdev, r/programming |
| **Comparison post** | "I compared [10 tools] for [task]. Here's the honest breakdown:" | Niche subreddits |
| **AMA** | "I'm a [role] who [achievement]. AMA" | Relevant subreddits |
| **Resource share** | "I created a free [resource] for [audience]. No signup required." | Niche subreddits |

| **Newsletter features** | Pitch to curators | 200-2K/feature | Low |

**Guest post pitch template:**
```
Subject: Post idea for [Publication]: [Working Title]

Hi [Editor],

I noticed [Publication] covers [topic]. I have a unique angle:

[Title]: [One-line summary]

Why this works for your audience:
- [Data point or unique insight you'll share]
- [Why it's different from what's been published]
- [Your credibility on this topic]

I've written for [1-2 relevant publications] and can turn this
around in [timeframe].

Happy to share an outline if interested.

[Your name]
```

### Engineering as Marketing (Free Tools)

Build a small, free tool that:
1. Solves a micro-problem your ICP has
2. Naturally leads to your paid product
3. Is shareable/linkable (earns SEO backlinks)

| Tool Type | Build Time | Traffic Potential | Example |
|-----------|:----------:|:-----------------:|---------|
| **Calculator** | 1-3 days | High (evergreen) | ROI calculator, pricing calculator |
| **Generator** | 2-5 days | High | Name generator, headline generator |
| **Analyzer/Auditor** | 3-7 days | Very High | Website grader, SEO auditor |
| **Template library** | 2-3 days | High | Free templates for [your niche] |
| **Comparison tool** | 2-5 days | High | Compare [tools/products] side-by-side |
| **Quiz/assessment** | 2-3 days | Very High | "What's your [score/type]?" |
| **Checker/validator** | 1-3 days | Medium | Email validator, link checker |

**Why this works:** Free tools earn backlinks naturally (200-1,000+ over time), drive ongoing organic traffic, and pre-qualify users for your paid product.

### LinkedIn Launch (B2B / SaaS)

**The "Build in Public" Launch Sequence:**

| Week | Post Type | Example |
|:----:|-----------|---------|
| -4 | Problem post | "The biggest problem I see with [industry] is..." |
| -3 | Journey post | "I've been working on something for the last [time]..." |
| -2 | Sneak peek | "Here's a preview of what I've been building..." |
| -1 | Behind-the-scenes | "Everything that went wrong building [product]" |

| +1 | First results | "24 hours since launch: [numbers]. Here's what I learned." |
| +2 | Customer story | "First customer feedback just came in..." |

### X/Twitter Launch

**Launch thread structure:**
```
Tweet 1 (Hook): "[Bold claim or result]. Here's how:"
Tweet 2: The problem (why this matters)
Tweet 3: What exists today (and why it's not enough)
Tweet 4: What [product] does differently
Tweet 5: Key feature #1 (with screenshot/demo)
Tweet 6: Key feature #2 (with screenshot/demo)
Tweet 7: Social proof (beta users, early results)
Tweet 8: The offer (pricing, free trial, etc.)
Tweet 9: CTA + link
Tweet 10: Engagement prompt ("What would you add?")
```

## Part 6: Paid Launch Amplification (When Ready)

### When to Add Paid (Prerequisites)

- [ ] Organic channels have proven the message resonates
- [ ] Landing page converts at 2%+ organically
- [ ] You understand your ICP deeply (from organic interactions)
- [ ] You have social proof (testimonials, numbers)
- [ ] You can afford to lose 2 months of ad spend while learning
- [ ] Your LTV > 3× expected CAC from paid

### Paid Channel Starter Guide

| Channel | Min Budget | Best For | Expected CPL |
|---------|:---------:|---------|:------------:|
| **Google Search** (branded + competitor) | $500/mo | High-intent capture | $20-50 |
| **Meta retargeting** | $300/mo | Website visitor conversion | $5-15 |
| **LinkedIn Ads** | $1,000/mo | B2B, specific job titles | $50-150 |
| **Twitter/X Ads** | $500/mo | Developer/startup audience | $10-30 |
| **Reddit Ads** | $300/mo | Niche communities | $5-20 |
| **Sponsorships** (newsletters) | $200-2K | Targeted audience | $10-50 |

**First $500 allocation (recommended):**
- $300: Retarget website visitors (Meta or Google Display)
- $200: Google Search on branded + competitor terms

## Part 7: The Launch Week Checklist

### Pre-Launch (Week Before)

- [ ] Product/landing page live and tested
- [ ] Email sequence loaded and scheduled
- [ ] Social content written and scheduled
- [ ] Product Hunt/HN submission prepared
- [ ] 10-15 launch supporters briefed
- [ ] Press/media outreach sent (if applicable)
- [ ] Analytics tracking configured (UTMs, goals, events)
- [ ] Customer support ready for volume
- [ ] FAQ page published

### Launch Day

- [ ] Product Hunt / HN submission live (12:01 AM PT)
- [ ] Email blast sent (morning, timezone-optimized)
- [ ] LinkedIn launch post published
- [ ] X/Twitter launch thread posted
- [ ] Reddit post in relevant subreddit (if earned reputation)
- [ ] Respond to every comment/question within 1 hour
- [ ] Share updates throughout the day
- [ ] Track real-time metrics (visitors, signups, feedback)

### Post-Launch (Week After)

- [ ] Thank everyone who supported
- [ ] Publish "launch results" post (transparent numbers)
- [ ] Follow up with every person who signed up
- [ ] Collect and respond to all feedback
- [ ] Fix top 3 issues raised during launch
- [ ] Write launch postmortem (what worked, what didn't)
- [ ] Plan next 30 days of post-launch marketing

## Part 8: Post-Launch Growth Channels

After the initial launch spike, sustain growth with:

| Channel | Time to Impact | Effort/Week | Compounding? |
|---------|:--------------:|:-----------:|:------------:|
| SEO / Content marketing | 3-6 months | 4-6 hours | Yes (strong) |
| Email nurture | 1-3 months | 1-2 hours | Yes (moderate) |
| LinkedIn personal brand | 1-3 months | 2-3 hours | Yes (strong) |
| Community participation | 2-4 months | 2-3 hours | Yes (moderate) |
| Partnerships/collabs | 2-4 weeks | 2-3 hours | No (one-time each) |
| Referral program | 2-6 months | 1 hour (after setup) | Yes (strong) |
| Product-led growth | 1-3 months | Engineering time | Yes (very strong) |

**The post-launch priority:** Double down on whatever channel generated the most engaged users during launch, not the most traffic. Quality > quantity.
