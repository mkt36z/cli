## Part 2: Workshop Facilitation Framework

### Pre-Workshop (1-2 Weeks Before)

| Step | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Define the outcome** | What decision or deliverable will this workshop produce? |
| **Invite the right people** | Decision makers + subject matter experts (3-8 people ideal) |
| **Send pre-work** | Questionnaire, reading material, or data to review |
| **Prepare materials** | Agenda, worksheets, reference data, sticky notes/whiteboard |
| **Set ground rules** | Share facilitation norms in advance |

### Facilitation Ground Rules

1. **Everyone speaks.** No observers — if you're in the room, you participate.
2. **One conversation at a time.** No side conversations.
3. **Time-boxed.** Every exercise has a hard stop. The facilitator is the timekeeper.
4. **Diverge then converge.** Generate ideas first, evaluate them second.
5. **Decisions are documented in real-time.** No "we'll figure it out later."
6. **Phones away.** Full presence required.
7. **No wrong answers in brainstorming.** Critique happens in the convergence phase.

## Part 3: Brand Strategy Workshop (Half-Day)

### Agenda

| Time | Activity | Duration | Output |
|:----:|---------|:--------:|--------|
| 0:00 | Welcome + objectives + ground rules | 10 min | Alignment |
| 0:10 | **Exercise 1: Brand Audit** — What does our brand stand for today? | 30 min | Current state map |
| 0:40 | **Exercise 2: Customer Empathy** — Who is our ideal customer and what do they actually care about? | 30 min | ICP profiles |
| 1:10 | Break | 10 min | — |
| 1:20 | **Exercise 3: Competitive Landscape** — Who else serves our customer and how are we different? | 30 min | Competitive positioning map |
| 1:50 | **Exercise 4: Brand Values** — What principles drive every decision? | 30 min | 3-5 core values |
| 2:20 | Break | 10 min | — |
| 2:30 | **Exercise 5: Brand Personality** — If our brand were a person, who would they be? | 20 min | Personality archetype + adjectives |
| 2:50 | **Exercise 6: Brand Promise** — What's the one thing we promise every customer? | 20 min | Brand promise statement |
| 3:10 | **Exercise 7: Messaging Hierarchy** — Build the messaging house | 30 min | Draft messaging hierarchy |
| 3:40 | Wrap-up: decisions, next steps, ownership | 20 min | Action items |

### Exercise Details

**Exercise 1: Brand Audit (Solo → Share)**
- Each participant writes 3 sticky notes: "Our brand is..." "Our brand is not..." "Our brand should be..."
- Group and discuss themes
- Identify gaps between "is" and "should be"

**Exercise 4: Brand Values (Dot Voting)**
- Present a list of 20 value words
- Each participant gets 5 votes
- Top 5-7 values advance to discussion
- Define each value with a "this means we..." statement
- Reduce to final 3-5

## Part 6: Post-Workshop Process

### The 48-Hour Rule

All workshop outputs must be documented and distributed within 48 hours. After that, momentum dies.

| Step | Timeline | Owner |
|------|:--------:|-------|
| Clean up notes and organize outputs | Same day | Facilitator |
| Draft the workshop summary document | Day +1 | Facilitator |
| Send summary to all participants for review | Day +2 | Facilitator |
| Collect any corrections or additions | Day +4 | Facilitator |
| Finalize deliverable and assign next steps | Day +5 | Account Director |

### Workshop Summary Template

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Workshop date and participants** | |
| **Objective** | What we set out to accomplish |
| **Key decisions made** | Numbered list of decisions |
| **Open questions** | Items that need further research |
| **Deliverables** | What will be produced from this workshop |
| **Next steps** | Who does what by when |

## Part 7: Facilitation Tips

| Tip | Why It Matters |
|-----|---------------|
| **Start with individual work, then share** | Prevents groupthink |
| **Use time-boxes aggressively** | Perfect is the enemy of done |
| **Capture decisions on a shared board** | Prevents "I thought we agreed on..." |
| **Assign a note-taker (not the facilitator)** | Facilitator needs to focus on the room |
| **End every exercise with "What did we just decide?"** | Forces explicit commitment |
| **Schedule breaks every 60-90 minutes** | Brain power fades without rest |
| **Limit to 3-8 participants** | More than 8 = too many cooks |
| **Send pre-work** | Participants come prepared = better output |
