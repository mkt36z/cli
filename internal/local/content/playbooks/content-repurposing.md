## The Core Principle

**Create once, distribute everywhere.** Every substantial piece of content should generate 10-25 derivative pieces across channels. This is how solo founders compete with teams of 10.

The repurposing system follows one rule: **adapt, don't copy-paste.** Each channel has native formats, expectations, and audience behavior. The same idea must be re-expressed in the language of each platform.

---

## Part 1: The Content Pyramid

```
                    ┌──────────────┐
                    │   PILLAR     │  1x per week/month
                    │  Long-form   │  Blog, podcast, video,
                    │  (Source)    │  newsletter, webinar
                  ┌─┴──────────────┴─┐
                  │   DERIVATIVE     │  5-10x per pillar
                  │   Mid-form       │  LinkedIn posts, email
                  │   (Adapted)      │  sequences, threads
                ┌─┴──────────────────┴─┐
                │     MICRO            │  10-25x per pillar
                │     Snackable        │  Tweets, quotes, carousels,
                │     (Atomic)         │  stories, short video clips
                └──────────────────────┘
```

### Pillar Content Types (Ranked by Repurposing Yield)

| Pillar Type | Repurposing Yield | Best For |
|------------|:-----------------:|---------|
| Long-form blog post (2000-4000 words) | 20-25 pieces | SEO + authority |
| Podcast/interview (30-60 min) | 15-25 pieces | Personality + depth |
| Webinar/workshop (45-90 min) | 20-30 pieces | Teaching + proof |
| Video (10-30 min) | 15-20 pieces | Visual learners |
| Newsletter issue (1000-2000 words) | 10-15 pieces | Audience nurture |
| Case study (1500-2500 words) | 15-20 pieces | Proof + social proof |

## Part 2: The Repurposing Matrix

For every pillar piece, extract and adapt into these formats:

### From a Blog Post / Long-Form Article

| Derivative | How to Extract | Channel |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| LinkedIn post (insight) | Pull the #1 contrarian take or framework | LinkedIn |
| LinkedIn carousel | Turn the step-by-step section into slides | LinkedIn |
| Twitter/X thread | Extract 5-7 key points as a narrative thread | X/Twitter |
| Email newsletter | Summarize + add personal context + CTA | Email |
| Short-form video (60-90s) | Record yourself explaining the core insight | TikTok, Reels, Shorts |
| Quote graphics (3-5) | Pull the most quotable sentences | Instagram, LinkedIn, X |
| Infographic | Visualize data, frameworks, or processes | Pinterest, LinkedIn |
| Community post | Frame as a question or discussion starter | Slack, Discord, Reddit |
| Podcast talking point | Use as a segment topic in your next episode | Podcast |
| Email sequence (3-part) | Expand key sections into a drip series | Email |

### From a Podcast / Interview

| Derivative | How to Extract | Channel |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| Audiogram clips (30-60s) | Best soundbites with waveform visual | Instagram, LinkedIn, X |
| Full transcript → blog post | Edit transcript into readable article | Blog/SEO |
| Key quotes (5-10) | Pull the most insightful or surprising statements | Social (all) |
| Thread of lessons learned | "5 things I learned from talking to [guest]" | X/Twitter |
| LinkedIn post per insight | One post per major topic covered | LinkedIn |
| Email with top 3 takeaways | "I just recorded an episode on [topic]. Here are 3 things..." | Email |
| Short-form video clips (3-5) | Best 60-second segments with captions | TikTok, Reels, Shorts |

### From a Case Study

| Derivative | How to Extract | Channel |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| Before/after LinkedIn post | "[Customer] went from [X] to [Y]. Here's how:" | LinkedIn |
| Twitter proof thread | Results + process + lesson | X/Twitter |
| Testimonial graphic | Key quote with headshot and result | Social (all) |
| Email social proof | Include in nurture sequence or launch emails | Email |
| Ad creative | Before/after with CTA | Paid ads |
| Landing page section | Add to social proof block | Landing page |
| Community success story | Share in relevant communities (with permission) | Slack, Reddit |

## Part 3: The Repurposing Process (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Create the Pillar (or Identify Existing Content)
- Write the blog post, record the podcast, deliver the webinar
- If repurposing existing content: audit your top 10 performing pieces first

### Step 2: Extract the Atomic Units

Read through the pillar and highlight:

| Atomic Unit Type | What to Look For | Target Count |
|-----------------|-----------------|:------------:|
| **Key insight** | Surprising fact, contrarian take, or "aha" moment | 3-5 per pillar |
| **Framework/process** | Step-by-step, numbered list, or matrix | 1-3 per pillar |
| **Story/anecdote** | Personal experience, customer story, or analogy | 2-4 per pillar |
| **Data point** | Statistic, benchmark, or specific result | 2-5 per pillar |
| **Quotable line** | Memorable phrasing someone would screenshot | 3-5 per pillar |
| **Question** | Thought-provoking question for engagement | 2-3 per pillar |

### Step 3: Adapt to Each Channel

**Critical rule:** Never copy-paste across channels. Each adaptation must:

| Channel | Adaptation Requirements |
|---------|----------------------|
| **LinkedIn** | Hook in first 2 lines, personal POV, line breaks, engagement question, 3-5 hashtags |
| **X/Twitter** | <280 chars per tweet, hook in first 7 words, thread structure if >1 tweet |
| **Email** | Personal tone, single CTA, P.S. line with proof or urgency |
| **Blog/SEO** | Keyword-optimized, proper H2/H3 structure, internal links, meta description |
| **Short-form video** | Hook in first 3 seconds, captions required, vertical format |
| **Carousel** | 1 idea per slide, visual hierarchy, CTA on last slide |
| **Community** | Conversational, ask for input, no self-promotion tone |

### Step 4: Schedule the Distribution

**The Drip Calendar** — Don't publish all derivatives at once:

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 | Publish pillar content (blog/podcast/video) |
| Day 0 | Send email to list with link + personal context |
| Day 1 | LinkedIn post (key insight #1) |
| Day 2 | Twitter/X thread (framework or process) |
| Day 3 | Short-form video (core insight, 60s) |
| Day 4 | LinkedIn carousel (framework/steps) |
| Day 5 | Community post (question or discussion) |
| Day 7 | LinkedIn post (key insight #2 or different angle) |
| Day 10 | Email (3-part sequence starts) |
| Day 14 | Reshare best-performing derivative with new hook |
| Day 30 | Recycle: repost best-performer with updated context |

## Part 4: The Content Recycling System

### The Evergreen Rotation

Content doesn't die after one post. High-performing pieces should be recycled:

| Cycle | When | What to Do |
|-------|------|-----------|
| **First run** | Day 0-14 | Full derivative distribution |
| **First recycle** | Day 30-45 | Repost top 3 performers with new hooks |
| **Quarterly refresh** | Every 90 days | Update stats, add new proof, repost |
| **Annual update** | Every 12 months | Full rewrite with updated data and examples |

### Performance-Based Recycling Rules

| Performance Signal | Action |
|-------------------|--------|
| Post got 2x average engagement | Recycle in 2-3 weeks with new hook |
| Post got 5x average engagement | Turn into a pillar piece (expand it) |
| Post generated leads/sales | Add to "Greatest Hits" rotation (monthly repost) |
| Post underperformed | Try different hook/angle, then retire if still flat |

## Part 5: The Content Audit Checklist

Before repurposing, audit existing content to find hidden gold:

### Step 1: Pull Your Top Performers

| Platform | Metric to Sort By | Top 10 |
|----------|-------------------|--------|
| Blog | Organic traffic, time on page | List top 10 URLs |
| Email | Open rate, click rate | List top 10 subject lines |
| LinkedIn | Impressions, engagement rate | List top 10 posts |
| X/Twitter | Impressions, engagement | List top 10 tweets |
| YouTube | Views, watch time | List top 10 videos |

### Step 2: Identify Repurposing Opportunities

For each top performer, ask:
1. Has this been adapted for other channels? (If no → repurpose it)
2. Is the data/info still current? (If no → update and repost)
3. Could this become a pillar piece? (If yes → expand it)
4. Does this contain proof/results? (If yes → priority repurposing)

## Part 6: Batch Production System

### The Weekly Content Batch (Solo Founder Workflow)

| Block | Duration | Activity |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Create** | 2-3 hours | Write/record ONE pillar piece |
| **Extract** | 30 min | Highlight atomic units, draft derivatives |
| **Adapt** | 1-2 hours | Channel-native adaptation for 5-7 derivatives |
| **Schedule** | 30 min | Queue everything in scheduling tool |
| **Total** | 4-6 hours/week | Produces 1 pillar + 10-15 derivative pieces |

### The Monthly Content Sprint (For Bigger Batches)

| Day | Activity | Output |
|-----|---------|--------|
| Day 1 | Create 4 pillar pieces (or record batch) | 4 pillar pieces |
| Day 2 | Extract atomic units from all 4 | 40-60 atomic units |
| Day 3 | Adapt to primary channel (LinkedIn or email) | 15-20 derivatives |
| Day 4 | Adapt to secondary channels (X, video, carousel) | 20-30 derivatives |
| Day 5 | Schedule everything for the month | Full month queued |

### Content Engine Protocol Addition

When producing content, the Content Engine should:
1. **Check if a pillar piece already exists** on this topic before writing from scratch
2. **Tag every pillar piece** with its repurposing status (fully repurposed / partially / not yet)
3. **Generate derivative briefs** alongside every new pillar piece
4. **Follow the Drip Calendar** for distribution timing
5. **Track recycling eligibility** based on performance data
