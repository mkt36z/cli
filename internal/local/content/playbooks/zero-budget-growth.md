## Tactic 1: Direct Warm Outreach ($0, Highest ROI for First 10 Customers)

This is the single most effective zero-budget tactic for getting your first paying customers.

### The Warm-First Outreach Protocol

**Step 1: List your warm network (aim for 100+ names)**
- Former colleagues
- LinkedIn 1st connections
- Friends/family in target industry
- People you've helped in the past
- Newsletter subscribers
- Conference contacts
- Community members who know you

**Step 2: Score each contact**
| Dimension | Score |
|-----------|:-----:|
| Fit (would they benefit from your product?) | 1-5 |
| Relationship strength | 1-5 |
| Influence (could they refer others?) | 1-5 |
| Total (reach out to 15+ first) | /15 |

**Step 3: Send the "Not Selling" Message**
```
Hey [Name],

I just built [one sentence about product]. I'm not selling anything —
I'm looking for honest feedback from [specific people like them].

Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week? I'll share what I'm
building and would genuinely love your unfiltered thoughts.

[Your name]
```

**Step 4: On the call, use the Mom Test framework**
- Don't pitch. Ask about their problems.
- "What's the hardest part about [their problem]?"
- "How are you solving that now?"
- "What have you tried that didn't work?"
- "If something could fix this, what would it look like?"
- Only at the end: "Would you want to try [product]?"

**Expected results:** 30-40% response rate → 50% take the call → 20-30% become users/customers. 100 contacts → 30-40 calls → 6-12 first customers.

## Tactic 2: Content SEO ($0, Highest Compounding Return)

### The Solo Founder Content Engine

**Publish 1 high-quality article per week. Just one. But make it the best answer on the internet for that topic.**

**Content selection framework (pick topics that are):**
- Searched by your ICP (use free keyword tools: Google autocomplete, AnswerThePublic, AlsoAsked)
- Within your genuine expertise (you can write from experience)
- Not dominated by massive authority sites
- Connected to your product's value proposition

**The 10x Content Formula:**
1. Google your target keyword
2. Read the top 5 results
3. Note what they all include
4. Note what they all MISS
5. Write something that's 10x more useful: deeper, more specific, more actionable, with original examples
6. Add original data, frameworks, or tools they don't have
7. Make it so good people bookmark and share it

**Free SEO tools for $0 budget:**
- Google Search Console (rankings, clicks, impressions)
- Google Analytics 4 (traffic, behavior)
- Google Keyword Planner (search volume estimates)
- Google Autocomplete + People Also Ask (keyword ideas)
- AnswerThePublic (question mining)
- Ubersuggest (limited free queries)

## Tactic 4: Community Participation ($0, Trust-Building at Scale)

### Where to Show Up

| Platform | How to Find Communities | Time Investment |
|----------|----------------------|:--------------:|
| Reddit | r/[your niche], r/startups, r/SaaS, r/Entrepreneur | 30 min/day |
| Indie Hackers | Groups, discussions, milestones | 20 min/day |
| Slack communities | Industry-specific Slack groups | 20 min/day |
| Discord servers | Niche professional communities | 15 min/day |
| Facebook groups | Largest groups in your niche | 15 min/day |
| Quora | Questions in your expertise area | 15 min/day |

### The 90/10 Rule
- 90% of your participation = genuinely helpful answers, no product mention
- 10% = subtle product reference ONLY when genuinely relevant

### Community Engagement Playbook
1. **Week 1-4:** Pure listening. Understand the culture. Note common questions.
2. **Week 5-8:** Start answering questions. Be the most helpful person in the thread.
3. **Week 9-12:** Share your own insights, frameworks, experiences (still no selling).
4. **Week 13+:** Your reputation precedes you. People start asking about your work.

**The "help first" framework for community responses:**
```
1. Acknowledge their specific situation
2. Give your best advice (don't hold back)
3. Share a resource (blog post, tool, template — yours or someone else's)
4. Offer to help further if they need it
5. (Only if relevant) Mention your product as ONE option among several
```

## Tactic 7: Launch Platforms ($0)

### Product Hunt Launch Protocol (free)

**Pre-launch (2 weeks):**
- [ ] Build a "coming soon" page collecting emails
- [ ] Reach out to 50+ Product Hunt community members
- [ ] Prepare 10+ screenshots and a demo GIF
- [ ] Write a compelling maker comment (the real pitch)
- [ ] Line up 5-10 people to comment authentically on launch day

**Launch day:**
- [ ] Launch at 12:01 AM PT (most time on the front page)
- [ ] Share on all social channels
- [ ] Email your list
- [ ] Respond to every comment within 30 minutes
- [ ] Ask supporters to share (not upvote — PH flags that)

### Show HN (Hacker News) Protocol

**Best practices:**
- Title format: "Show HN: [Product] – [One line description]"
- Comment with a detailed explanation of what you built and why
- Be honest about limitations
- Respond thoughtfully to every comment
- Post between 8-10 AM ET on weekdays

## The "Time is Money" Framework

Even at $0 budget, your time has an opportunity cost. Maximize ROI per hour:

| Activity | Expected Hours | Expected Outcome | ROI Score |
|----------|:-------------:|-----------------|:---------:|
| Warm outreach | 1 hour → 3-4 conversations | Direct revenue opportunity | 10/10 |
| SEO content | 4 hours → 1 article | Compounds for years | 9/10 |
| LinkedIn posting | 30 min → 1 post | Builds pipeline | 8/10 |
| Community help | 30 min → 3-5 answers | Trust building | 7/10 |
| Cold DMs | 1 hour → 20 messages | 2-3 conversations | 5/10 |
| Social scrolling | 1 hour → nothing | Zero impact | 0/10 |

**The solo founder time allocation (10 hours/week for marketing):**
- 3 hours: Content creation (1 article OR 5 LinkedIn posts)
- 3 hours: Direct outreach + conversations
- 2 hours: Community participation
- 1 hour: Email list nurture
- 1 hour: Partnership outreach

## When to Start Spending Money

You've earned the right to invest in paid channels when:
- [ ] You have 10+ paying customers from organic/free channels
- [ ] You understand your CAC from organic (rough estimate)
- [ ] Your LTV is at least 3x your expected paid CAC
- [ ] You have a landing page converting at 2%+ organically
- [ ] You have proof points (testimonials, case studies, data)
- [ ] You can afford to lose 2 months of ad spend while learning

**First paid dollar should go to:** Retargeting your existing website traffic (cheapest, highest-intent audience you already have).
