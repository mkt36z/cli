## Part 1: PR Strategy Foundation

### PR vs. Marketing: Know the Difference

| Dimension | PR | Marketing |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| **Goal** | Earned media, reputation, credibility | Leads, revenue, direct response |
| **Control** | Low — journalists decide what to publish | High — you control the message |
| **Trust** | Very high — third-party endorsement | Medium — self-promotion |
| **Cost** | Time-intensive, relationship-driven | Budget-driven, scalable |
| **Timeline** | Slow build, compounding impact | Can be immediate |
| **Measurement** | Media mentions, SOV, backlinks, referral traffic | Leads, revenue, CAC |

### When PR Matters Most

| Situation | PR Priority |
|-----------|:---:|
| Fundraising (need press for credibility) | Critical |
| Launch (need awareness at scale) | High |
| Category creation (need to frame the narrative) | Critical |
| Hiring (need employer brand) | Medium |
| Ongoing (brand building, thought leadership) | Medium |
| Crisis (need to control narrative) | Critical |

## Part 2: Building Your PR Foundation

### Step 1: Define Your Story

Every company has multiple stories. Identify which ones resonate with media:

| Story Type | Formula | When It Works |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **Founder story** | "I experienced [problem] and built [solution]" | Early-stage, human interest |
| **Trend story** | "Here's what's happening in [industry] and what it means" | Riding a wave, thought leadership |
| **Data story** | "We analyzed [X] and found [surprising insight]" | Original research, contrarian data |
| **David vs. Goliath** | "We're taking on [giant] because [reason]" | Challenger brands, disruption |
| **Milestone story** | "[Achievement] — and here's how we got there" | Fundraising, growth, partnerships |
| **Customer impact** | "[Customer] achieved [remarkable result] using [product]" | Case study with newsworthy outcomes |

### Step 2: Build Your Media List

| Category | Who | How to Find | Your List |
|----------|-----|-----------|-----------|
| **Tier 1 — Dream targets** | 5-10 journalists who cover your exact space | Read their recent articles | |
| **Tier 2 — Industry press** | 10-20 journalists at trade publications | Industry publication mastheads | |
| **Tier 3 — Local / regional** | 5-10 local business journalists | Local newspaper, business journal | |
| **Tier 4 — Podcasters** | 10-15 podcast hosts in your space | Apple Podcasts, Spotify search | |
| **Tier 5 — Newsletters** | 5-10 newsletter writers with relevant audiences | Substack, Beehiiv search | |

## Part 3: The Pitch

### Pitch Structure (The Zitron Method)

```
Subject: [Specific, newsworthy hook — not "Check out our startup"]

Hi [First Name],

[1 sentence: Why THIS journalist, referencing their recent work]

[1-2 sentences: The news/story — what happened and why it matters NOW]

[1-2 sentences: Supporting data or context that makes it interesting]

[1 sentence: What's available — interview, data, exclusive, embargo offer]

[Sign-off with full contact info]
```

### Pitch Examples

**Funding announcement:**
```
Subject: [Company] raises $XM to [solve problem] for [who]

Hi [Name],

Loved your piece on [related topic] last week — particularly the point about
[specific detail].

[Company] just closed a $XM [round] led by [investor] to [solve problem] for
[target market]. We're seeing [data point that validates market demand].

Happy to share details, customer stories, or make our CEO available for a
15-min call. Would an exclusive work for your timeline?
```

**Trend/thought leadership:**
```
Subject: Data: [X%] of [group] are now [surprising behavior]

Hi [Name],

Given your coverage of [topic], thought you'd find this interesting:

We surveyed [X number] of [audience] and found that [surprising finding].
This challenges the conventional wisdom that [previous assumption].

I can share the full data set + our CEO's take on what it means for [industry].
Would a 10-minute call work this week?
```

### Pitch Rules

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Personalize every pitch (reference their work) | Don't mass-email from a template |
| Lead with the story, not your company | Don't lead with "I'm reaching out from..." |
| Keep it under 150 words | Don't write a novel — journalists scan |
| Offer something specific (data, exclusive, interview) | Don't say "let me know if you're interested" |
| Follow up once (after 3-5 days) | Don't follow up more than twice |
| Pitch on Tuesday-Thursday mornings | Don't pitch on Mondays or Fridays |

## Part 4: Ongoing PR Tactics

### The PR Calendar

| Frequency | Activity | Purpose |
|:---------:|---------|---------|
| Weekly | Monitor media for relevant stories to react to | Newsjacking opportunities |
| Weekly | Engage with target journalists on social media | Relationship building |
| Bi-weekly | Submit to HARO / Qwoted / journalist requests | Expert source positioning |
| Monthly | Pitch 2-3 journalists with original story angles | Proactive media coverage |
| Quarterly | Publish original research / data report | Thought leadership + earned links |
| Annually | Major press push (funding, launch, milestone) | Flagship coverage |

### Reactive PR (Newsjacking)

When a relevant story breaks:

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|------|--------|:--------:|
| 1 | Identify the story and its relevance to your expertise | Same day |
| 2 | Draft a hot take or expert perspective (200 words) | Same day |
| 3 | Email 3-5 journalists covering the story | Same day |
| 4 | Post your take on LinkedIn / X | Same day |
| 5 | Offer yourself as an expert source for follow-up pieces | Day +1 |

## Part 5: Measuring PR Impact

### PR Metrics

| Metric | How to Track | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Media mentions** | Google Alerts, Mention, Meltwater | Brand awareness in press |
| **Share of voice** | Mentions vs. competitors | Market perception position |
| **Domain authority / backlinks** | Ahrefs, Moz | SEO impact of earned media |
| **Referral traffic from PR** | Google Analytics (referral source) | Direct audience impact |
| **Social amplification** | Social shares of press coverage | Reach beyond the publication |
| **Message pull-through** | Did they use our key messages? | Message effectiveness |
| **Lead attribution** | "How did you hear about us?" → press | Business impact |

## Part 6: Common PR Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Pitching everyone, personalizing no one | Research the journalist. Reference their work. |
| "We're excited to announce..." | Lead with the story, not the company emotion |
| Pitching before you have a story | A press release is not a story. A story has tension, insight, or surprise. |
| Expecting immediate results | PR is a relationship game. 3-6 months to build momentum. |
| Only doing PR during launches | Ongoing relationship-building creates readiness for when you need coverage |
| Treating journalists as marketing channels | They're professionals with their own agenda. Respect it. |
