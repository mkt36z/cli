# Playbook: Creative Direction — Structured Creativity for Marketing

## Why This Playbook Exists
Most marketing output is mediocre because teams rely on instinct instead of systematic creative processes. The top 1% of creative directors use structured methodologies to consistently produce breakthrough work. This playbook encodes 20+ creative thinking frameworks, evaluation rubrics calibrated against world-class standards, and processes that turn "I need an idea" into "Here are 10 ideas, ranked by impact."

*Sources: SIT (Systematic Inventive Thinking), TRIZ (Theory of Inventive Problem Solving), Bisociation (Arthur Koestler), SCAMPER (Bob Eberle), Synectics (William Gordon), Lateral Thinking (Edward de Bono), and evaluation frameworks from Cannes Lions, D&AD, and HumanKind.*

## Part 1: Creative Ideation Methodologies

### 1.1 SCAMPER — The Universal Adaptation Engine

Apply to ANY existing marketing asset to generate 7+ variations:

| Letter | Technique | Questions to Ask | Marketing Application |
|:------:|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **S** | Substitute | What can we replace? What alternative material, process, or approach? | Swap the channel, format, audience, offer, or messenger |
| **C** | Combine | What can we merge? What ideas, features, or formats? | Combine a webinar with a product demo; merge two audiences |
| **A** | Adapt | What can we borrow from elsewhere? What parallel exists? | Adapt a B2C viral technique for B2B; borrow from entertainment |
| **M** | Modify/Magnify | What can we enlarge, shrink, change frequency, or intensity? | 10x the specificity; shrink a 30-min webinar to 5-min video |
| **P** | Put to other uses | Where else could this work? New audience, channel, or context? | Turn a blog post into a LinkedIn carousel, email course, or tool |
| **E** | Eliminate | What can we remove to simplify? What's unnecessary? | Strip a landing page to headline + CTA only; remove the pitch from content |
| **R** | Reverse/Rearrange | What if we flip the order, perspective, or assumption? | Show the result first, then explain how; sell the problem, not the solution |

**Exercise:** Take your best-performing marketing piece. Apply all 7 SCAMPER techniques. You'll generate at least 7 fresh variations in under 30 minutes.

### 1.2 SIT — Systematic Inventive Thinking (5 Innovation Templates)

SIT constrains the problem space to force creative breakthroughs. Counterintuitively, constraints increase creativity.

#### Template 1: Subtraction
**Principle:** Remove a core component and find value in its absence.

| Element Removed | Marketing Innovation |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Pricing page | "Name your own price" or "Pay what it's worth" |
| Sales team | Fully self-serve PLG motion |
| Written content | Video-only or audio-only content strategy |
| Brand name in ads | Anonymous testimonial-led campaigns |
| Free trial | "Earn your trial" through engagement (scarcity) |

#### Template 2: Multiplication
**Principle:** Copy a component but change it in a meaningful way.

| Component Multiplied | Innovation |
|---------------------|-----------|
| CTA | Multiple CTAs, each for different awareness levels |
| Headline | A/B/C/D test with 4 radically different angles |
| Landing page | Personalized variants by industry, role, or stage |
| Content format | Same idea as blog, video, podcast, infographic, tool |
| Price tier | 5 tiers instead of 3 (including a "symbolic" $1 tier) |

#### Template 3: Division
**Principle:** Break something into parts and reorganize.

| Original | Divided Into | Value Created |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Sales page | 5-email sequence (each section = 1 email) | Dripped value, reduced overwhelm |
| Feature list | One feature per week (feature spotlights) | Ongoing engagement, not one-time |
| Annual report | 12 monthly micro-reports | Consistent content cadence |
| Pricing | Modular pricing (pay per feature) | Lower barrier, higher customization |
| Ebook | 10-post LinkedIn series | Distribution on platform vs. gated |

#### Template 4: Task Unification
**Principle:** Assign new functions to existing components.

| Existing Component | New Task Assigned | Innovation |
|-------------------|------------------|-----------|
| Customer support | Content creation (FAQ → blog posts) | Support-driven content engine |
| Product usage data | Marketing content (usage insights → case studies) | Product-led content |
| Email unsubscribe page | Feedback collection + retention offer | Win-back at point of churn |
| 404 page | Lead capture + product showcase | Recover lost traffic |
| Pricing page | Competitive comparison + ROI calculator | Conversion + education |

#### Template 5: Attribute Dependency
**Principle:** Create relationships between previously independent variables.

| Variable A | Variable B | Dependency Created |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Price | Usage | Usage-based pricing |
| Content depth | Engagement score | Progressive content unlocking |
| Email frequency | Engagement level | Adaptive send cadence |
| Ad creative | Time of day | Dayparted messaging |
| Landing page | Traffic source | Source-specific personalization |

### 1.3 TRIZ — The Inventive Problem-Solving System

TRIZ identifies 40 inventive principles for solving contradictions. The most applicable to marketing:

| # | Principle | Marketing Application |
|---|-----------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Segmentation** | Divide market into micro-segments with personalized messaging |
| 2 | **Taking out** | Remove the part of marketing that doesn't work (e.g., remove forms for low-friction signup) |
| 5 | **Merging** | Combine complementary products/offers into bundles |
| 10 | **Preliminary action** | Pre-educate before the sales conversation (content marketing) |
| 13 | **The other way round** | Let customers sell to each other (community, UGC) |
| 15 | **Dynamics** | Make static assets dynamic (personalized, real-time, adaptive) |
| 17 | **Another dimension** | Add a new channel, format, or sensory element |
| 22 | **Blessing in disguise** | Turn a weakness into a marketing advantage ("We're expensive because...") |
| 25 | **Self-service** | Product does the marketing itself (PLG, virality) |
| 28 | **Replace mechanical** | Replace human processes with automated/AI (what Marketing36z OS does) |
| 35 | **Parameter changes** | Change the unit of measurement (price per user → price per result) |
| 40 | **Composite materials** | Combine different content types into new hybrid formats |

### 1.4 Bisociation — Cross-Domain Creative Thinking (Koestler)

**The core idea:** Breakthrough creativity comes from connecting two previously unrelated frames of reference ("matrices of thought").

**The Bisociation Exercise (20 minutes to 10+ ideas):**

1. **Define your marketing challenge** (e.g., "How do we make our SaaS product feel essential")
2. **Pick a random domain** (nature, cooking, sports, music, architecture, medicine, military, gaming)
3. **List 10 concepts from that domain** (e.g., from cooking: recipe, seasoning, mise en place, knife skills, taste test, sous chef, plating, comfort food, fusion, farm-to-table)
4. **Force connections** to your challenge:
   - Recipe → "Your marketing recipe" (framework as step-by-step recipe)
   - Mise en place → "Prep everything before you create" (strategy before execution)
   - Taste test → "Try before you buy" (free trial framing)
   - Comfort food → "The marketing tool that feels like home" (familiarity positioning)
   - Farm-to-table → "First-party data to first-party marketing" (data-driven positioning)
5. **Select the 3 strongest connections** and develop into campaign concepts

### 1.5 Lateral Thinking Techniques (De Bono)

| Technique | How It Works | Marketing Exercise |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Random Entry** | Start from a random word/image, force connections | Open a dictionary, connect the word to your product |
| **Provocation (PO)** | Make an intentionally absurd statement, extract value | "PO: What if our product was free forever?" → freemium model |
| **Challenge** | Question every assumption | "Why do we need a website?" → app-first or community-first |
| **Reversal** | Do the opposite of conventional wisdom | Instead of "attract customers," "repel non-customers" |
| **Analogy** | Find a parallel in a different field | "Our product is like a GPS for marketing decisions" |
| **Fractionation** | Break the problem into smallest possible parts | "What's the #1 micro-moment where we lose customers?" |

---

### 1.6 Synectics — Making the Familiar Strange

| Technique | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Direct Analogy** | Find a parallel in nature or another field | "Our AI is like a personal trainer — it adapts to your level" |
| **Personal Analogy** | Become the product/customer | "If I WERE the email, what would I want to say?" |
| **Symbolic Analogy** | Use metaphors and compressed descriptions | "Marketing36z is a Swiss Army knife for founders" |
| **Fantasy Analogy** | Wish fulfillment without constraints | "What if marketing did itself while you sleep?" |

## Part 2: Creative Evaluation Frameworks

### 2.1 The 6-Axis Creative Scorecard

Evaluate any creative work across these 6 dimensions (calibrated against Cannes Lions / D&AD / HumanKind standards):

| Axis | 1 (Weak) | 2 (Below Average) | 3 (Competent) | 4 (Strong) | 5 (Exceptional) |
|------|---------|------------------|--------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Originality** | Derivative, seen many times | Minor twist on common approach | Fresh angle within category | Novel approach, stands out | Category-defining, never seen |
| **Relevance** | Disconnected from audience | Loosely related to their world | Addresses a real need | Deeply resonates with ICP | Feels like it was made just for them |
| **Craft** | Sloppy execution | Passable quality | Professionally done | Polished, attention to detail | Exquisite, every element perfect |
| **Impact** | No emotional response | Mild interest | Memorable for a day | Hard to forget, shareable | Changes how they think about the category |
| **Strategy** | Off-brand, off-target | Loosely connected to goals | Aligned with strategy | Advances the strategic narrative | Redefines the strategic conversation |
| **Effectiveness** | Won't drive action | Might generate awareness | Will drive measurable results | Will significantly move metrics | Will transform business trajectory |

**Scoring:**
- **25-30:** Ship immediately — this is exceptional work
- **20-24:** Strong — minor refinements then ship
- **15-19:** Competent — meets standards but won't break through. Iterate.
- **10-14:** Below average — needs significant rework
- **Below 10:** Start over — fundamental issues

### 2.2 The "Would I Share This?" Test

Before any piece ships, ask:
1. **Would I send this to a friend?** (If no → not interesting enough)
2. **Would I screenshot this for later?** (If no → not valuable enough)
3. **Would I comment on this?** (If no → not provocative enough)
4. **Would I remember this tomorrow?** (If no → not distinctive enough)
5. **Would I buy because of this?** (If no → not persuasive enough)

**Rule:** At least 3 of 5 must be "yes" before publishing.

### 2.3 Creative Kill Criteria

**Kill the idea if:**
- It requires explanation — great creative is self-evident
- You can swap in any competitor's name and it still works
- It leads with features, not feelings or outcomes
- The only thing going for it is "it's clever"
- It can't be summarized in one sentence
- It violates the brand's voice or values
- It doesn't have a clear, measurable outcome attached

## Part 3: The Creative Process (5 Phases)

### Phase 1: Brief (15% of time)
- Define ONE objective (not three)
- Identify the single target audience
- Write the one thing you want them to think/feel/do
- Set constraints (budget, timeline, format, channel)
- **Output:** 1-page brief

### Phase 2: Diverge (30% of time)
- Generate 50+ ideas (quantity over quality)
- Use at least 3 different ideation techniques (SCAMPER, SIT, Bisociation, etc.)
- No judgment during this phase — all ideas are valid
- Encourage wild, impractical ideas (they inspire practical ones)
- **Output:** Raw idea list (50+)

### Phase 3: Converge (20% of time)
- Score top 20 ideas against the 6-Axis Scorecard
- Select top 5 for development
- Apply the Kill Criteria filter
- **Output:** 3-5 finalist concepts

### Phase 4: Develop (25% of time)
- Flesh out top 3 concepts into full executions
- Create rough drafts, wireframes, or storyboards
- Test internally (team reactions, gut check)
- **Output:** 3 developed concepts with rationale

### Phase 5: Refine (10% of time)
- Select the winner
- Polish craft and execution details
- Apply the "Would I Share This?" test
- Get stakeholder approval
- **Output:** Final creative, ready to ship

## Part 4: Creative Direction for Solo Founders

### The "Good Enough" Creative Framework

Solo founders don't have time for a 5-phase creative process on every piece. Here's the compressed version:

**The 15-Minute Creative Sprint:**
1. **What's the ONE thing?** (30 seconds — the single message)
2. **Who cares?** (30 seconds — the specific person this is for)
3. **SCAMPER spin** (5 minutes — apply 3 SCAMPER techniques to generate variations)
4. **Pick the best** (2 minutes — gut check + would-I-share test)
5. **Write it** (7 minutes — draft, don't polish)

**The "B+ and Ship" Rule:**
- Perfection is the enemy of momentum
- B+ work published today beats A+ work published next month
- Volume + speed + iteration > one perfect piece
- Save your creative energy for cornerstone content (1-2 pieces/month)

### Creative Swipe File Categories

Keep a swipe file organized by:

| Category | What to Save | Where to Find |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Headlines that stopped you** | Screenshot + note on why | Emails, ads, social |
| **Landing pages that converted you** | Full screenshot + URL | Products you've purchased |
| **Emails you actually read** | Full email + subject line | Your inbox |
| **Ads you clicked** | Screenshot + platform | Social feeds, search |
| **Social posts you saved** | Screenshot + engagement data | Bookmarks |
| **Stories that moved you** | Key structure + emotional beats | Anywhere |
| **Visuals that stood out** | Image + context + why | Ads, social, websites |

**Rule:** Add 3-5 items weekly. Review monthly. Patterns will emerge.

## Part 5: Cross-Channel Creative Consistency

### The Creative Cascade

One big idea → adapted for every channel:

| Channel | Adaptation Rules | Duration/Format |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Blog/SEO** | Most complete version, SEO-optimized, educational | 2,000-5,000 words |
| **Email** | Personal, story-driven, one CTA | 300-500 words |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional insight, framework-oriented | 200-300 words + visual |
| **X/Twitter** | Sharpest single insight, thread for depth | 280 chars or 5-10 tweet thread |
| **Instagram** | Visual-first, carousel for education | 10-slide carousel or 30-60s reel |
| **TikTok** | Raw, personality-led, trend-adapted | 15-60 second video |
| **YouTube** | Comprehensive, tutorial-style or story | 5-15 minutes |
| **Ad creative** | Benefit-led, proof-stacked, CTA-driven | 15-30 second video or static image |
| **Podcast** | Conversational, story-rich, interview-style | 20-45 minutes |

**Consistency rules across channels:**
1. Same core message, different expression
2. Same brand voice, different register (formal/casual)
3. Same visual identity, platform-native format
4. Same CTA destination, different entry points
5. Same proof points, different emphasis per audience

## Quick Reference: Methodology Selection Guide

| Situation | Best Methodology | Why |
|-----------|-----------------|-----|
| Need variations of existing asset | SCAMPER | Systematic modification of what exists |
| Stuck — no ideas at all | Random Entry / Bisociation | Forces unexpected connections |
| Need to innovate a process | SIT (5 Templates) | Structural innovation within constraints |
| Need to challenge assumptions | Lateral Thinking | Questions fundamentals |
| Need emotional/metaphorical concepts | Synectics | Deep analogical thinking |
| Need to solve a contradiction | TRIZ | Resolves "we want X but also Y" |
| Evaluating multiple options | 6-Axis Scorecard | Objective, calibrated evaluation |
| Quick creative for daily content | 15-Minute Sprint | Speed over perfection |
