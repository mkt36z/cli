# Signal-Based Marketing Playbook

## What Is Signal-Based Marketing?

Signal-based marketing replaces batch-and-blast campaigns with targeted, triggered actions based on behavioral, intent, and contextual signals. Instead of sending the same email to everyone on Tuesday, you send the right message to the right person at the moment they signal readiness.

**The shift:** Broadcast → Triggered. Calendar-driven → Signal-driven. Spray-and-pray → Precision.

## Part 1: Signal Types Taxonomy

### Five Signal Categories

#### 1. Intent Signals
Behaviors that indicate active interest in solving a problem you address.

| Signal | Strength | Source | Decay Rate |
|--------|:--------:|--------|:----------:|
| Visited pricing page (2+ times) | Very High | Website analytics | 7 days |
| Downloaded comparison guide | High | Content analytics | 14 days |
| Searched for competitor name + "alternative" | High | SEO tools, Google Ads | 7 days |
| Read 3+ blog posts in one week | Medium | Website analytics | 14 days |
| Watched product demo video (>50%) | High | Video analytics | 14 days |
| Visited careers page of competitor | Medium | Intent data providers | 30 days |
| Visited G2/Capterra comparison page | Very High | Review site partnerships | 7 days |

#### 2. Trigger Signals
External events that create buying windows.

| Signal | Strength | Source | Action Window |
|--------|:--------:|--------|:-------------:|
| New VP/Director of Marketing hired | Very High | LinkedIn, job change alerts | 1-4 weeks |
| Company raised funding round | High | Crunchbase, press | 2-6 weeks |
| Competitor acquired/merged | Medium | News monitoring | 2-4 weeks |
| Company expanded to new market | Medium | Press, LinkedIn | 2-6 weeks |
| Key executive departed competitor | High | LinkedIn alerts | 1-2 weeks |
| Contract renewal approaching | Very High | CRM data | 90 days before |
| Fiscal year / budget cycle start | Medium | Industry knowledge | 30 days before |

#### 3. Engagement Signals
Interactions with your brand that indicate interest level.

| Signal | Strength | Source | Decay Rate |
|--------|:--------:|--------|:----------:|
| Email reply (not just open) | Very High | Email platform | 14 days |
| Attended webinar/event | High | Event platform | 30 days |
| Community active (asked question) | High | Community platform | 14 days |
| Social media engagement (comment) | Medium | Social monitoring | 7 days |
| Returned to site after 30+ day absence | Medium | Website analytics | 14 days |
| Opened 5+ emails in a month | Medium | Email platform | 30 days |
| Downloaded multiple assets | High | Content analytics | 14 days |

#### 4. Fit Signals
Characteristics that indicate alignment with your ICP.

| Signal | Strength | Source | Decay Rate |
|--------|:--------:|--------|:----------:|
| Company size matches ICP | High | Firmographic data | Stable |
| Industry matches target vertical | High | Firmographic data | Stable |
| Uses complementary technology | Medium | BuiltWith, technographic data | 90 days |
| Uses competitor technology | Very High | Technographic data | 90 days |
| Growth rate above threshold | Medium | LinkedIn, funding data | 90 days |
| Located in target geography | Medium | Firmographic data | Stable |

#### 5. Timing Signals
Contextual cues about when a prospect is most receptive.

| Signal | Strength | Source | Action Window |
|--------|:--------:|--------|:-------------:|
| End of quarter (buying urgency) | Medium | Calendar | Last 2 weeks of Q |
| Industry conference season | Medium | Event calendars | 2 weeks before/after |
| Competitor price increase announced | High | Competitive intel | 2-4 weeks |
| Regulatory deadline approaching | High | Industry knowledge | 60-90 days before |
| Seasonal business peak | Medium | Industry knowledge | 30 days before |

## Part 2: Signal Scoring Methodology

### Signal Strength Formula

```
Signal Score = (Strength × 3) + (Recency × 2) + (Frequency × 1)
```

| Factor | 1 (Low) | 2 (Medium) | 3 (High) |
|--------|---------|------------|----------|
| **Strength** | Informational interest | Active evaluation | Purchase intent |
| **Recency** | 30+ days ago | 7-30 days ago | Within 7 days |
| **Frequency** | One-time | 2-3 occurrences | 4+ occurrences |

**Score interpretation:**
| Score Range | Classification | Action |
|:-----------:|:-------------:|--------|
| 15-18 | Hot | Immediate personal outreach |
| 10-14 | Warm | Accelerated nurture + sales alert |
| 6-9 | Developing | Standard nurture sequence |
| 1-5 | Cold | Monitor, no outreach |

## Part 3: Signal-to-Action Playbooks

### Play 1: Job Change Play
**Signal:** New VP/Director of Marketing hired at target account
**Why it works:** New leaders have a 90-day mandate to make changes. They're actively evaluating tools and approaches.

| Day | Action | Channel | Owner |
|:---:|--------|---------|:-----:|
| 1-3 | Congratulate on LinkedIn (genuine, no pitch) | LinkedIn | SDR |
| 7 | Send "Welcome to [role]" email with relevant content | Email | Marketing |
| 14 | Share industry benchmark report relevant to their company | Email | Marketing |
| 21 | Request connection meeting — "help you get up to speed" | LinkedIn + Email | SDR |
| 30 | If engaged: offer strategy session or assessment | Email/Call | AE |

### Play 2: Funding Round Play
**Signal:** Target company raised Series A/B/C
**Why it works:** Fresh capital = budget for growth tools. Growth is the mandate.

| Day | Action | Channel | Owner |
|:---:|--------|---------|:-----:|
| 1-3 | Congratulate founder/CEO on social media | LinkedIn/X | SDR |
| 7 | Send "Growth Playbook for Post-Funding Companies" | Email | Marketing |
| 14 | Invite to relevant webinar or community | Email | Marketing |
| 21 | Personal outreach with case study of similar-stage company | Email | SDR |
| 30 | If engaged: propose growth assessment | Call | AE |

### Play 3: Technology Adoption Play
**Signal:** Target installed competitor's product
**Why it works:** They've identified the need but may not have chosen the right solution.

| Day | Action | Channel | Owner |
|:---:|--------|---------|:-----:|
| 1-7 | Add to "competitive displacement" nurture | Email | Marketing |
| 14 | Send comparison guide (yours vs. competitor) | Email | Marketing |
| 21 | Share case study of customer who switched | Email | Marketing |
| 30 | Personal outreach: "How's [competitor] working out?" | LinkedIn | SDR |
| 45 | If engaged: offer competitive assessment | Call | AE |

### Play 4: Content Consumption Play
**Signal:** Prospect read 3+ blog posts in one week
**Why it works:** They're actively researching your problem space.

| Trigger | Action | Channel | Owner |
|---------|--------|---------|:-----:|
| 3 posts in 7 days | Send related pillar content ("You might also like...") | Email | Marketing |
| Pricing page visit | Alert SDR for personalized follow-up | Slack/CRM | SDR |
| Return visit within 48hr | Show targeted CTA/popup (case study, demo offer) | Website | Marketing |
| Demo video watched | Fast-track to sales-assist | Email/Call | SDR |

### Play 5: Community Activity Play
**Signal:** Prospect asked question in community about problem you solve
**Why it works:** They've self-identified a need. Help first, sell never.

| Step | Action | Channel | Owner |
|:----:|--------|---------|:-----:|
| 1 | Respond helpfully (NO pitch) | Community | Community Manager |
| 2 | Follow up with additional resources | DM | Community Manager |
| 3 | If they engage: offer to connect with an expert | DM | SDR |
| 4 | If qualified: transition to product conversation | Call/Email | AE |

**Rule:** Value-first, always. If someone asked for help in a community and you pitch them, you'll damage your brand.

### Play 6: Pricing Page Play
**Signal:** Visited pricing page 2+ times in 7 days
**Why it works:** They're evaluating cost — they're close to a decision.

| Trigger | Action | Channel | Owner |
|---------|--------|---------|:-----:|
| 1st pricing visit | Add to retargeting audience | Ads | Marketing |
| 2nd pricing visit (7 days) | Send ROI calculator or case study | Email | Marketing |
| 3rd pricing visit | Alert sales for personal outreach | Slack/CRM | SDR |
| If ICP match | Offer custom pricing or consultation | Email/Call | AE |

### Play 7: Competitor Vulnerability Play
**Signal:** Competitor has major outage, negative press, or pricing backlash
**Why it works:** Competitor's customers are actively reconsidering.

| Day | Action | Channel | Owner |
|:---:|--------|---------|:-----:|
| 1-3 | Publish thought leadership (empathetic, not gloating) | Blog/Social | Content Engine |
| 3-7 | Create comparison content | Blog/Landing page | Content Engine |
| 7-14 | Targeted ads to competitor's audience | Paid Social/Search | Channel Operator |
| 14+ | Personal outreach to known competitor accounts | Email/LinkedIn | SDR |

**Rule:** Never gloat. Be empathetic and helpful. "We know switching is hard — here's how we make it easy."

### Play 8: Renewal Timing Play
**Signal:** Contract with competitor coming up for renewal
**Why it works:** Renewal windows are the most natural switching points.

| Timeline | Action | Channel | Owner |
|:--------:|--------|---------|:-----:|
| 90 days before | Begin soft engagement (content, community invites) | Email/Community | Marketing |
| 60 days before | Send case study of similar company that switched | Email | Marketing |
| 45 days before | Offer competitive assessment or migration plan | Email | SDR |
| 30 days before | Personal outreach with switching incentive | Call/Email | AE |
| 15 days before | Final push: limited-time migration support offer | Email | AE |

## Part 4: Signal Stacking

### Combining Multiple Weak Signals into Strong Intent

Individual signals can be ambiguous. Stacking multiple signals creates high-confidence intent.

**Signal Stack Scoring:**

| Combination | Score | Classification |
|------------|:-----:|:-------------:|
| Job change + pricing page visit + content download | 15+ | Hot — immediate action |
| Funding round + 3 blog posts + community join | 12+ | Warm — accelerated nurture |
| ICP fit + email engagement + social follow | 8+ | Developing — standard nurture |
| Single signal only | 3-6 | Cold — monitor |

**Rule:** Act on signal stacks, not individual signals. One blog post visit means nothing. Three blog posts + pricing page + from an ICP-fit company = worth pursuing.

## Part 6: Privacy & Ethics

### 5 Principles for Ethical Signal-Based Marketing

1. **Transparency:** Be clear about what data you collect and how you use it
2. **Relevance:** Only act on signals relevant to genuine value you can provide
3. **Value-first:** Every signal-triggered action must offer value to the recipient
4. **Opt-out respect:** Honor unsubscribes and "not interested" signals immediately
5. **Data minimization:** Only collect signals you'll actually act on

### Compliance Considerations

| Regulation | Impact on Signal-Based Marketing |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| GDPR | Legitimate interest basis for B2B; consent for email marketing |
| CCPA/CPRA | Right to know, delete; don't sell personal data |
| CAN-SPAM | Unsubscribe in every email, accurate headers |
| CASL (Canada) | Express consent required for commercial email |

## Part 7: Measuring Signal-Based Marketing Effectiveness

| Metric | How to Calculate | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------------|:---------:|
| **Signal-to-meeting rate** | Meetings booked / signals acted on | >5% |
| **Signal-to-pipeline rate** | Pipeline $ / total signals | Increasing |
| **Response rate by play** | Responses / outreach per play type | >10% for warm plays |
| **Time to action** | Average time from signal to first touch | <48 hours |
| **Signal accuracy** | % of signals that were genuine buying signals | >30% |
| **Revenue influenced** | Revenue from signal-triggered campaigns | Growing as % of total |
| **Play conversion rates** | Pipeline per play type | Track and optimize |

## Key Takeaways

1. **Signals > schedules.** Triggered campaigns outperform batch campaigns 3-5x.
2. **Stack signals for confidence.** Multiple weak signals = one strong signal.
3. **Speed matters.** Act within 48 hours of a signal or it decays.
4. **Value first, pitch never.** Every signal-triggered action must help the prospect.
5. **Start simple.** You don't need expensive tools — Google Alerts and LinkedIn alerts get you 70% there.
6. **Measure by play.** Track conversion rates per play type and double down on winners.
7. **Respect privacy.** Ethical signal-based marketing builds trust; creepy targeting destroys it.
