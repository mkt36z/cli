## Part 1: The Core Principle

> **Hire for the stage you're at, not the org chart you dream about.**

Most companies either hire too senior too early (expensive, bored, leaves) or too junior too early (cheap, overwhelmed, underperforms). This playbook maps the right team structure to your revenue stage.

## Part 2: Marketing Org by Revenue Stage

### Stage 1: Pre-Revenue to $500K ARR (Founder + 0-1 Marketer)

**Who does marketing:** The founder. Period.

| Role | Who | Focus |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Chief Marketer** | Founder | Positioning, messaging, first 100 customers, content |
| **First Marketing Hire** (optional) | Generalist (T-shaped) | Content execution, email, social, analytics |

**First hire profile:** Someone who can write, understands basic analytics, and ships fast. NOT a strategist. NOT a specialist. A generalist who produces.

**What Marketing36z OS replaces at this stage:**
- Strategy Planner → positioning, messaging, competitive analysis
- Content Engine → copywriting, content production
- Channel Operator → channel selection and execution guides
- Quality Guardian → review and QA

### Stage 2: $500K — $2M ARR (2-4 Person Team)

| Role | Focus | Key Metric |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **Head of Marketing** (or senior generalist) | Strategy, positioning, pipeline | Marketing-sourced pipeline |
| **Content Marketer** | Blog, SEO, social, newsletter | Organic traffic + leads |
| **Growth / Demand Gen** | Paid, email, conversion optimization | CAC + MQLs |
| **Designer** (part-time / freelance) | Brand, landing pages, ads | Design velocity |

**Org structure:**
```
Founder (brand + strategy oversight)
  └── Head of Marketing
        ├── Content Marketer
        ├── Growth / Demand Gen
        └── Freelance Designer
```

### Stage 3: $2M — $10M ARR (5-10 Person Team)

| Function | Roles | Focus |
|----------|-------|-------|
| **Brand & Content** | Content Lead, Writer, Designer | Thought leadership, SEO, brand |
| **Demand Gen** | Demand Gen Lead, Campaign Manager | Pipeline, paid, email, events |
| **Product Marketing** | PMM | Positioning, launches, sales enablement |
| **Ops / Analytics** | Marketing Ops | Tech stack, attribution, reporting |

**Org structure:**
```
VP Marketing / CMO
  ├── Brand & Content
  │     ├── Content Lead
  │     ├── Writer(s)
  │     └── Designer
  ├── Demand Generation
  │     ├── Demand Gen Lead
  │     └── Campaign Manager
  ├── Product Marketing
  │     └── PMM
  └── Marketing Ops
        └── Marketing Analyst
```

### Stage 4: $10M — $50M ARR (10-25 Person Team)

| Function | Team Size | Key Additions |
|----------|:---------:|-------------|
| **Brand & Content** | 4-6 | Video producer, social media manager |
| **Demand Gen** | 3-5 | ABM specialist, event manager |
| **Product Marketing** | 2-4 | Competitive intel, customer marketing |
| **Marketing Ops** | 2-3 | Data engineer, marketing technologist |
| **Comms / PR** | 1-2 | PR manager, internal comms |

### Stage 5: $50M+ ARR (25+ Person Team)

| Function | Sub-Functions |
|----------|-------------|
| **Brand** | Brand strategy, creative studio, brand partnerships |
| **Content** | Editorial, SEO, social media, video/podcast |
| **Demand Generation** | Paid, email, ABM, events, partnerships |
| **Product Marketing** | PMM by product line, competitive intel, analyst relations |
| **Customer Marketing** | Advocacy, community, expansion, onboarding content |
| **Marketing Ops** | Analytics, tech stack, attribution, data, compliance |
| **Communications** | PR, internal comms, corporate narrative, crisis |
| **International** | Regional marketing, localization |

## Part 3: The Hiring Sequence

### The "What to Hire When" Framework

| Hire # | Role | Why Now | Don't Hire Before |
|:------:|------|---------|------------------|
| 1 | **Content Generalist** | You need volume — blog, email, social | You have positioning + messaging nailed |
| 2 | **Demand Gen / Growth** | You need scalable acquisition | You have product-market fit |
| 3 | **Designer** (freelance → FT) | Brand consistency matters at scale | You have enough content to design for |
| 4 | **Product Marketing Manager** | Sales needs enablement, launches need structure | You have a sales team |
| 5 | **Marketing Ops / Analyst** | Data is getting complex, tech stack needs management | You have 3+ channels running |
| 6 | **Head of Content / Brand** | Content function needs a dedicated leader | You have 2+ content producers |
| 7 | **PR / Comms** | Market presence matters, media opportunities arise | You have a story the market cares about |
| 8 | **Customer Marketing** | Retention and expansion become critical | You have 100+ customers |

### The T-Shaped Marketer Model

For early hires, look for T-shaped marketers:

```
Broad knowledge across all channels (the top of the T)
|————————————————————————————————————————————|
         |
         | Deep expertise in 1-2 areas (the stem of the T)
         |
         |
```

| Deep Expertise Area | Best Fit For |
|--------------------|-------------|
| Content / SEO | Content-led growth companies |
| Paid / Performance | Companies with ad budget and clear unit economics |
| Product / Technical | Developer tools, complex B2B |
| Brand / Creative | Consumer-facing, design-driven products |
| Community / Social | Community-led or PLG companies |

## Part 4: Marketing Team Operating System

### Meeting Cadence

| Meeting | Frequency | Duration | Attendees | Purpose |
|---------|:---------:|:--------:|-----------|---------|
| **Daily standup** | Daily | 10 min | Whole team | Blockers, priorities |
| **Weekly metrics** | Weekly | 30 min | Whole team | Review dashboards, adjust |
| **Campaign review** | Bi-weekly | 45 min | Campaign owners | Deep dive on active campaigns |
| **Content calendar** | Weekly | 30 min | Content team | Plan next week's content |
| **Strategy review** | Monthly | 60 min | Leadership + marketing | Performance, strategy, budget |
| **Quarterly planning** | Quarterly | Half-day | Whole team | OKRs, themes, resource allocation |

### Decision-Making Framework

| Decision Type | Who Decides | Who's Consulted | Examples |
|--------------|------------|----------------|---------|
| **Day-to-day execution** | Individual contributor | Nobody needed | Social post copy, email subject line |
| **Campaign launch** | Campaign owner | Head of Marketing | New ad campaign, email sequence |
| **Budget allocation** | Head of Marketing | Finance, CEO | Channel spend reallocation |
| **Brand / positioning** | VP Marketing + CEO | Product, sales | Messaging changes, rebrand |
| **Hiring** | Hiring manager + VP | Team, HR | New role, contractor vs. FT |
| **Tool / vendor** | Marketing Ops | Users of the tool | New SaaS purchase, agency hire |

### The RACI for Common Marketing Activities

| Activity | Content | Demand Gen | PMM | Ops | Head of Mktg |
|----------|:-------:|:----------:|:---:|:---:|:------------:|
| Blog post | R/A | C | C | — | I |
| Product launch | C | C | R/A | C | A |
| Paid campaign | — | R/A | C | C | I |
| Sales collateral | C | — | R/A | — | I |
| Attribution report | — | C | — | R/A | A |
| Brand guidelines | R/A | I | C | — | A |
| Event | C | R/A | C | C | A |

*R = Responsible, A = Accountable, C = Consulted, I = Informed*

## Part 5: Agency vs. In-House vs. Freelance

### The Build-Buy Matrix

| Capability | In-House | Freelance | Agency | Why |
|-----------|:--------:|:---------:|:------:|-----|
| **Strategy / positioning** | ✅ | | | Core competency, don't outsource |
| **Content writing** | | ✅ | | Scale flexibly, find niche experts |
| **SEO technical** | | ✅ | ✅ | Specialized skill, episodic need |
| **Paid media management** | | | ✅ | Requires tools + ongoing optimization |
| **Design / creative** | | ✅ | | Scale as needed, cheaper than FT |
| **PR / media relations** | | | ✅ | Requires relationships and rolodex |
| **Video production** | | ✅ | ✅ | Specialized equipment and skills |
| **Marketing ops / analytics** | ✅ | | | Needs deep institutional knowledge |
| **Social media management** | ✅ | ✅ | | Voice consistency requires proximity |

### When to Bring In-House

A function should move in-house when:
1. It's a core differentiator (your content voice IS your brand)
2. You need it daily, not monthly
3. The cost of freelance/agency equals an FT hire
4. Institutional knowledge creates compounding returns
5. Speed of iteration matters more than polish
