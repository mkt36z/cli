## Part 1: Communications Architecture

| Layer | Audience | Purpose | Owner |
|-------|---------|---------|-------|
| **External — PR & Media** | Journalists, analysts, public | Brand reputation, media coverage | PR / Comms |

## Part 2: Corporate Narrative

### Building the Corporate Narrative

The corporate narrative is the overarching story that connects your mission, vision, strategy, and customer impact.

| Element | Content | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Origin story** | Why the company exists — the founder's insight | "We saw that [problem] was costing [audience] [pain], and nobody was fixing it." |
| **Mission** | What you do and for whom | "We [action] for [audience] so they can [outcome]." |
| **Vision** | The future you're building toward | "A world where [desired state]." |
| **Strategic narrative** | The market shift you're riding | "The world is moving from [old way] to [new way]. We enable [new way]." |
| **Values in action** | How your values show up in real decisions | "[Value] means we [specific action], even when [it's hard because]." |
| **Proof** | Evidence that your narrative is real | Metrics, customer stories, milestones |

### The Narrative Hierarchy

```
Corporate Narrative (CEO keynote, investor deck, about page)
    │
    ├── Product Narrative (product marketing, launches)
    │
    ├── Customer Narrative (case studies, testimonials)
    │
    ├── Employer Narrative (careers page, Glassdoor, recruiting)
    │
    └── Thought Leadership Narrative (POV, content, speaking)
```

**Rule:** Every sub-narrative must be consistent with and derived from the corporate narrative. If your product marketing says one thing and your careers page says another, you have a narrative fracture.

## Part 3: Internal Communications

### The Internal Comms Calendar

| Cadence | Format | Audience | Content |
|---------|--------|---------|---------|
| **Weekly** | Email or Slack digest | All employees | Key wins, updates, recognition |
| **Bi-weekly** | Team standup notes | Cross-functional | Progress, blockers, priorities |
| **Monthly** | All-hands meeting | All employees | Company metrics, strategy, Q&A |
| **Quarterly** | Strategy update | All employees | OKR review, next quarter plan |
| **Annually** | Year in review | All employees + external | Accomplishments, vision, gratitude |
| **As needed** | Announcement | Relevant audience | New hires, policy changes, milestones |

### All-Hands Meeting Template (Monthly, 45 min)

| Segment | Time | Content | Owner |
|---------|:----:|---------|-------|
| Welcome + company pulse | 5 min | Key metrics, quick wins | CEO |
| Strategic update | 10 min | Progress on company priorities | CEO / Exec |
| Department spotlight | 10 min | Deep dive on one team's work | Department lead |
| Customer story | 5 min | Real customer impact | CS / Sales |
| Recognition | 5 min | Shout-outs, awards, celebrations | HR / CEO |
| Q&A | 10 min | Open questions (anonymous option) | CEO |

### Internal Announcement Template

```
📢 [Announcement Type: New Hire / Policy Change / Milestone / etc.]

[One-sentence headline summary]

What's happening:
[2-3 sentences explaining the change/news]

Why it matters:
[1-2 sentences on impact and context]

What you need to do:
[Specific actions, if any. "No action needed" is a valid answer.]

Questions?
[Who to contact or where to ask]
```

## Part 4: Investor and Board Communications

### Investor Update Template (Monthly/Quarterly)

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Headline metric** | MRR/ARR, growth rate, key milestone |
| **Key wins** | 3-5 bullet points on top achievements |
| **Key challenges** | 1-2 honest challenges and how you're addressing them |
| **Financial summary** | Revenue, burn rate, runway |
| **Key metrics table** | MRR, growth, churn, CAC, LTV, team size |
| **Product update** | Major releases, roadmap progress |
| **Team update** | Key hires, departures, org changes |
| **Asks** | Specific ways investors can help (intros, advice, hiring) |

### Board Deck Structure (Quarterly)

| Slide | Content |
|:-----:|---------|
| 1 | Executive summary (3-5 bullet points) |
| 2-3 | Financial performance vs. plan |
| 4-5 | Key metrics deep dive (growth, retention, unit economics) |
| 6 | Product update and roadmap |
| 7 | Go-to-market update (marketing, sales, partnerships) |
| 8 | Team and organizational update |
| 9 | Competitive landscape |
| 10 | Key risks and mitigations |
| 11 | Priorities for next quarter |
| 12 | Discussion topics / asks |

## Part 6: Communication Quality Standards

### The 5 C's of Effective Communication

| C | Definition | Test |
|---|-----------|------|
| **Clear** | Easy to understand | Can a new employee understand this? |
| **Concise** | No unnecessary words | Can you cut 30% without losing meaning? |
| **Consistent** | Aligned with narrative and past communications | Does this match what we've said before? |
| **Contextual** | Audience-appropriate | Is this right for who's reading it? |
| **Candid** | Honest, even when uncomfortable | Are we hiding anything? |

### Communication Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Impact | Fix |
|-------------|--------|-----|
| Corporate jargon | Nobody knows what you mean | Write like you speak |
| Burying bad news | Trust erosion when discovered | Lead with transparency |
| Over-communicating | Information fatigue | Signal-to-noise ratio matters — be selective |
| Under-communicating | Rumor mill fills the void | Regular cadence prevents anxiety |
| One-way broadcasting | Disengagement | Always include feedback channels |
