# Workflow: Offer Design Lab

## Purpose
A dedicated workflow for designing, testing, and refining offers that score high on the Hormozi Value Equation. This is arguably the most important workflow in the system — because the offer IS the marketing. A bad offer with great copy loses to a great offer with bad copy every time.

## When to Use This Workflow
- Founder is launching a new product or service
- Existing campaigns are underperforming (often an offer problem, not a copy problem)
- Strategy Planner's Value Equation score comes back Medium or Low
- Founder wants to raise prices but fears losing customers
- Competitor has made your offer commoditized

---

## Phase 1: Market Desire Discovery

### Step 1.1: Identify the Mass Desire (Schwartz)
Every successful offer channels a pre-existing mass desire. Find it.

**Questions to answer:**
- What does your audience already desperately want?
- What pain are they already spending money to solve?
- What emotional state are they trying to escape?
- What emotional state are they trying to reach?
- What identity do they want to claim?

**Sources to mine:**
- Amazon reviews of competitor products (3-star reviews are gold — they show both hopes and disappointments)
- Reddit/forum threads where the audience complains
- Sales call recordings — exact words prospects use
- Support tickets — what do customers struggle with?
- Survey data — open-ended responses, not just ratings

**Output:** A "Desire Map" — the top 3-5 mass desires ranked by intensity and urgency.

### Step 1.2: Identify the Dream Outcome
Not what the product DOES, but what the customer's life looks like AFTER.

**The Transformation Framework:**
```
FROM: [Current painful state — in their words]
TO:   [Dream state — in their words]
VIA:  [Your product/service — your mechanism]
```

**Rules:**
- Dream outcome must be stated in the customer's language
- Must be specific enough to visualize ("save time" is bad; "get your weekends back" is good)
- Must be emotionally resonant (connects to identity, status, freedom, or security)

## Phase 2: Offer Architecture (Hormozi Framework)

### Step 2.1: List Every Problem
List EVERY problem the customer encounters before, during, and after getting their dream outcome.

**The Problem Discovery Process:**
1. What problems do they face BEFORE they start? (awareness, trust, fear)
2. What problems do they face WHILE using the solution? (effort, confusion, time)
3. What problems do they face AFTER getting results? (maintaining, next level, new problems)

Create an exhaustive list. 20-50 problems is normal.

### Step 2.2: Turn Problems into Solutions
For each problem, create a solution. These become the components of your offer.

| Problem | Solution | Delivery Vehicle |
|---------|----------|-----------------|
| "I don't know where to start" | Step-by-step onboarding guide | Video course module |
| "I don't have time" | Done-for-you setup service | Service add-on |
| "What if it doesn't work for me?" | 30-day money-back guarantee | Guarantee |
| "I need accountability" | Weekly check-in calls | Group coaching |

### Step 2.3: Apply the Delivery Cube
For each solution, choose the optimal delivery method:

| Dimension | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| **Ratio** | 1-on-1 / Small group / 1-to-many |
| **Medium** | In-person / Phone / Email / Chat / Video / Software |
| **Level** | Do-it-yourself / Done-with-you / Done-for-you |
| **Effort** | High effort (highest value) / Medium / Low effort |
| **Consumption** | Live / Recorded / Written |
| **Speed** | Immediate / Fast / Normal |
| **Bonuses** | Included free / Paid add-on |

**Key insight:** Moving from DIY → DFY and from 1-to-many → 1-on-1 increases both value AND price potential.

### Step 2.4: Score on the Value Equation

```
           Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
```

Score each dimension 1-10:

| Dimension | Score | How to Improve |
|-----------|-------|---------------|
| Dream Outcome | /10 | Make it bigger, more specific, more emotional |
| Perceived Likelihood | /10 | Add proof, guarantees, case studies, demonstration |
| Time Delay | /10 | Faster first result, quick wins, milestone markers |
| Effort & Sacrifice | /10 | Remove steps, provide tools, do-it-for-them, templates |

**Total Value Score:** (DO × PL) / (TD × ES) = ___

**Target:** Score must be above 5.0 to proceed to copy. Below 5.0 → redesign the offer.

## Phase 3: Value Stacking

### Step 3.1: Design the Core Offer
The main product/service that delivers the dream outcome.

### Step 3.2: Design the Bonus Stack
Bonuses must:
- Address specific problems from Step 2.1
- Have a high perceived value relative to cost to deliver
- Be named specifically (not "Bonus 1" — give them outcome-oriented names)
- Each bonus should be something people would pay for individually

**Bonus Naming Formula:**
`[Specific Outcome] [Type of Asset]`
Examples: "The 7-Day Quick Start Blueprint" / "Done-For-You Campaign Templates" / "Weekly Expert Office Hours"

### Step 3.3: Value Stack Presentation
Present the offer so the total perceived value dramatically exceeds the price:

```
Core Offer: [Name] ...................... Value: $X,XXX
Bonus 1:    [Name] ...................... Value: $XXX
Bonus 2:    [Name] ...................... Value: $XXX
Bonus 3:    [Name] ...................... Value: $XXX
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Total Value: ............................ $XX,XXX
Your Price Today: ....................... $X,XXX (fraction of total)
```

**Rule:** Total perceived value should be 10-100x the price asked.

## Phase 4: Guarantee Design

### Guarantee Types (from weakest to strongest)

| Type | What It Says | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Implied** | "We stand behind our work" | Premium brands (use sparingly) |
| **Unconditional** | "Money back, no questions asked, X days" | Digital products, low marginal cost |
| **Conditional** | "If you do X and don't get Y, money back" | Service businesses, coaching |
| **Anti-guarantee** | "This is so good, all sales are final" | Ultra-premium, exclusivity |
| **Performance** | "If you don't get [specific result], we pay you $X" | High-confidence offers |
| **Outrageous** | "If it doesn't work, I'll fly to you and fix it personally" | Category disruption |

**How to choose:** Match guarantee aggressiveness to your offer confidence. Higher confidence → more aggressive guarantee → higher conversion.

## Phase 5: Urgency & Scarcity Design

### Legitimate Urgency Frameworks (never fabricate)

| Type | Example | When to Use |
|------|---------|------------|
| **Cohort-based** | "Next cohort starts March 15" | Courses, programs |
| **Seasonal** | "Q1 pricing available until March 31" | Seasonal services |
| **Capacity** | "We can only take 10 clients this month" | Service businesses |
| **Bonus deadline** | "These bonuses expire Friday at midnight" | Product launches |
| **Price increase** | "Price goes up when we hit 500 customers" | Early-stage products |
| **External event** | "Before the new regulation takes effect" | Compliance/regulatory |

**Rule:** Fake scarcity and fake urgency destroy trust permanently. Only use real constraints.

---

## Phase 6: Offer Naming

### The Naming Formula (Hormozi)
A great offer name should convey the result, the time frame, and the container.

**Formula:** `[Result] [Time Frame] [Container Type]`

Examples:
- "The 90-Day Revenue Accelerator"
- "6-Week Funnel Sprint"
- "The No-Code Launch System"

**Naming Checklist:**
- [ ] Does it communicate the dream outcome?
- [ ] Does it imply a timeline?
- [ ] Is it specific enough to visualize?
- [ ] Is it different from competitor naming?
- [ ] Would someone be proud to say "I'm in [name]"?

---

## Phase 7: Offer Testing Protocol

### Before Full Launch
1. **Gut check:** Would YOU buy this at this price? Be honest.
2. **5-person test:** Describe the offer to 5 ICPs. Do their eyes light up?
3. **Price sensitivity:** Test 3 price points with identical positioning.
4. **Objection capture:** What do the 5 people say? These become your copy angles.

### After Soft Launch (First 30 Days)
- Conversion rate by traffic source
- Most common objections from non-buyers
- Price elasticity (test higher prices — most founders underprice)
- Bonus engagement (which bonuses do people mention? Which do they ignore?)
- Refund rate (high refund = value delivery problem, not marketing problem)

---

## Output of This Workflow

A complete offer specification document:
1. Desire Map (mass desires ranked)
2. Transformation statement (FROM → TO → VIA)
3. Problem exhaustion list with solution mapping
4. Delivery cube decisions
5. Value Equation score (must be >5.0)
6. Value stack with perceived values
7. Guarantee design
8. Urgency/scarcity strategy (real constraints only)
9. Offer name
10. Testing protocol and success criteria

**This feeds directly into:** Campaign Brief Template (sections 6, 7, 8) → Content Engine → Channel Operator
