# Workflow: First 10 Customers Playbook

## Purpose
Guide pre-revenue and early-stage founders through the systematic process of acquiring their first 10 paying customers. This is the most critical milestone in any business — proof that someone will pay for what you've built. No marketing system matters until you've crossed this threshold.

## When to Use This Workflow
- Founder is pre-revenue with a product or service ready (or nearly ready) to sell
- Founder has revenue but acquired customers through luck/network, not a repeatable process
- Orchestrator identifies founder at **Stage Zero** or **Stage Early**
- Strategy Planner flags "no paying customers" or "no repeatable acquisition channel"

---

## Phase 1: Validate Before You Sell

Before chasing customers, confirm you're solving a real problem for a specific person.

### Step 1.1: The "Will They Pay?" Test

Don't build. Don't market. First, answer these:

| Question | How to Answer | Red Flag If... |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| Can you describe the problem in the customer's exact words? | VOC mining (reviews, forums, conversations) | You describe it in YOUR words, not theirs |
| Have 5+ people told you they'd pay for this? | Direct conversations (not surveys) | Only friends/family said yes |
| Does a budget already exist for this? | Research what they currently spend | They'd need to create a new budget line |
| Is the pain urgent or chronic? | Ask: "What happens if you don't solve this?" | "It would be nice" vs. "I'm losing money/time/sleep" |
| Can you reach these people directly? | List 20 names you could contact today | You can't name 20 real people |

**Decision gate:**
- 5/5 = Proceed to Phase 2
- 3-4/5 = Proceed with caution, flag gaps
- <3/5 = STOP. Use the PMF Validation workflow (`06-pmf-validation.md`) first

### Step 1.2: Define Your First Customer Profile

This is NOT your full ICP. This is your **First-10 Customer Profile** — a much narrower slice:

```
Who is the ONE type of person most likely to:
1. Feel this pain acutely RIGHT NOW
2. Have money to spend on a solution TODAY
3. Be reachable by you DIRECTLY (no ads, no SEO)
4. Be forgiving of an imperfect product
5. Become a vocal advocate if you deliver
```

## Phase 2: Build Your Prospect List (The Dream 20)

### Step 2.1: Create Your Hit List

You need exactly 20 names. Not 200. Not 2,000. Twenty real people who match your First-10 Profile.

**Sources for your Dream 20:**

| Source | How to Find Them | Expected Yield |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| Your existing network | LinkedIn connections, email contacts, past colleagues | 3-5 names |
| LinkedIn Search | Job title + company size + industry filter | 5-8 names |
| Communities you're already in | Slack groups, Discord, Reddit, forums | 3-5 names |
| Competitors' customers | Review sites, case studies, testimonials | 2-4 names |
| Referrals from first contacts | "Who else do you know who has this problem?" | 2-5 names |

### Step 2.2: Research Each Prospect (15 min each)

For each of your 20 prospects, document:

1. **Their specific situation** (what are they working on?)
2. **Their likely pain point** (based on your First-10 Profile)
3. **A warm-up angle** (mutual connection, shared community, their content you can reference)
4. **The best channel to reach them** (email, LinkedIn DM, mutual intro)

## Phase 3: The Outreach System

### Step 3.1: The Warm Outreach Framework (Hormozi)

**Rule:** Your first 10 customers come from warm outreach. Not ads. Not content. Not SEO. Direct, personal conversations.

#### The 3-Message Sequence

**Message 1: The Value-First Touch** (Day 1)
```
Subject: [Specific observation about their situation]

Hey [Name],

I noticed [specific thing about them — their post, their company, their challenge].

I've been working on [your domain] and just put together [a resource/insight/tool]
that specifically addresses [their likely pain point].

Would it be useful if I shared it with you? No strings attached.

[Your name]
```

**Message 2: The Insight Share** (Day 3-5, after they respond)
```
Here's [the resource]. I built this because I kept seeing [the pattern/problem]
and realized most [their role] are stuck doing [current painful approach].

Curious — is [specific version of the problem] something you're dealing with too?
```

**Message 3: The Soft Offer** (Day 7-10, only if they engaged)
```
Based on what you shared, I think I can help with [their specific situation].

I'm working with a small group of [role/type] to [solve this specific problem].
Here's what it looks like: [1-2 sentence description of your offer].

Would it make sense to spend 15 minutes exploring if this is a fit?
```

### Step 3.2: The Conversation Framework

When you get on a call, follow this structure:

| Phase | Duration | What to Do | What NOT to Do |
|-------|----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Discovery** | 10-15 min | Ask about their situation, pain, what they've tried | Don't pitch. Don't talk about your product. |
| **Diagnosis** | 5 min | Summarize their problem back to them in their words | Don't use jargon or your framework names |
| **Prescription** | 5 min | Explain how you'd solve their specific problem | Don't give a generic demo or feature tour |
| **Close** | 5 min | Make a specific offer with clear next steps | Don't say "let me know" or "think about it" |

#### The Close Script
```
"Based on everything you've shared, here's what I'd recommend:

[Specific solution for their specific problem]

The investment is [price]. That includes [what they get].

If you don't see [specific result] within [timeframe],
[your guarantee — refund, free extension, etc.].

Would you like to get started?"
```

### Step 3.3: Handling the First-Customer Pricing Dilemma

| Approach | When to Use | Script |
|----------|------------|--------|
| **Founding member rate** | You want paying customers but need social proof | "I'm offering a founding member rate of [50-70% of target price] for my first 10 customers. You'll lock in this rate permanently, and I'll ask for your honest feedback as I refine the offering." |
| **Pay-what-you-want** | You're not sure of pricing yet | "I want to prove this works for you. Pay what you think it's worth after 30 days." |
| **Full price with guarantee** | You're confident in the value | "The investment is [full price]. If you don't see [result] in [timeframe], I'll refund 100%." |
| **Beta access exchange** | You need case studies more than revenue | "I'm offering free access to my first 5 users in exchange for a detailed case study and testimonial." |

**Rule:** Never give it away for free without getting something in return (feedback, testimonial, case study, referral commitment).

## Phase 4: From Customer 1 to Customer 10

### Step 4.1: The First-Customer Delight Protocol

Your first customer is your most important marketing asset. Over-deliver:

| Action | Timeline | Why |
|--------|----------|-----|
| Personal onboarding call | Day 1 | Set expectations, build relationship |
| Daily check-in (first week) | Days 1-7 | Catch problems early, show you care |
| Weekly check-in | Weeks 2-4 | Track progress toward promised outcome |
| Celebrate their first win | As soon as it happens | Create an emotional anchor to your product |
| Ask for specific feedback | Week 2 | "What's one thing I should change?" |
| Document their results | Week 4 | This becomes your first case study |

### Step 4.2: The Referral Multiplier

After delivering a result for Customer 1:

```
"[Name], I'm thrilled that [specific result they achieved].

I'm looking for 2-3 more [their role/type] who are dealing with
[the same problem you solved for them].

Who comes to mind?"
```

**Expected conversion:** Each happy customer should refer 1-2 qualified prospects. This is how you go from 1 → 3 → 7 → 10.

### Step 4.3: The Proof Snowball

Each customer creates proof that makes the next sale easier:

| Customer # | Proof You Now Have | How to Use It |
|------------|-------------------|---------------|
| 1 | First testimonial, first result | Add to outreach messages |
| 2-3 | Pattern emerges (consistent results) | Create a mini case study |
| 4-5 | Social proof volume ("5 founders...") | Add to landing page |
| 6-7 | Diverse results across use cases | Create comparison stories |
| 8-10 | Statistical confidence | "9 out of 10 customers saw [result]" |

## Phase 5: Extract the Playbook

Once you have 10 customers, document:

### The Acquisition Debrief

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Where did most customers come from? | (Source/channel) |
| What message/angle resonated most? | (Specific words/framing) |
| What was the average sales cycle? | (Days from first touch to purchase) |
| What was the #1 objection? | (And how you overcame it) |
| What was the conversion rate? | (Conversations → customers) |
| What was the average deal size? | ($) |
| What's the #1 reason they bought? | (In their words) |
| What would you do differently? | (Lessons learned) |

### Feed Back Into the System
- Update ICP with validated First-10 Profile data → Strategy Planner
- Add customer language to the Language Bank → Content Engine
- Create case studies from best results → Case Study Template
- Use proven messaging in first content pieces → Content Repurposing Workflow

## Metrics & Kill Criteria

| Metric | Target | Kill Criteria |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Prospect list built | 20 names in 2 days | Can't find 20 → ICP too narrow or non-existent market |
| Response rate on outreach | >30% reply rate | <10% after 20 messages → messaging problem |
| Conversation rate | >50% of replies → call booked | <25% → value proposition unclear |
| Close rate | >25% of calls → customer | <10% after 10 calls → offer or pricing problem |
| Time to 10 customers | 30-90 days | >120 days → fundamental product-market fit issue |

## Common Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

| Mistake | Why It Happens | What to Do Instead |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Building before selling | Fear of rejection | Sell the outcome, then build the delivery |
| Targeting everyone | Fear of missing out | Pick ONE First-10 Profile and commit |
| Leading with features | Founder excitement | Lead with the problem and the outcome |
| Discounting to zero | Desperation | Charge something — even $1 proves willingness to pay |
| Scaling outreach too fast | Impatience | Perfect the message with 20 before trying 200 |
| Ignoring early feedback | Ego | Your first customers are telling you what to build next |
| Not asking for referrals | Awkwardness | It's the #1 growth lever at this stage — always ask |
