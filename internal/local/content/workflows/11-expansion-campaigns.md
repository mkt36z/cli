# Workflow: Expansion Campaign Playbook

## Purpose
A structured workflow for running campaigns that grow revenue from existing customers — upsells, cross-sells, and tier upgrades. Expansion revenue is 3-5x cheaper than new customer acquisition and is the fastest path to improving unit economics.

## When to Use This Workflow
- Net revenue retention (NRR) is below 100%
- Existing customers have unused capacity or underutilized features
- New features or tiers have launched
- Customer usage data indicates expansion readiness
- Orchestrator routes here for "expansion," "upsell," or "cross-sell" requests

## Phase 1: Expansion Opportunity Identification

### Step 1.1: Expansion Signal Detection

| Signal | Source | What It Means | Expansion Play |
|--------|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Hit usage limit** | Product analytics | They need more capacity | Tier upgrade |
| **Added team members** | Product analytics | Growing team, growing need | Seat expansion |
| **Using advanced features** | Product analytics | Power user, ready for pro | Feature-tier upgrade |
| **High NPS score** | Survey | Happy customer, open to more | Cross-sell, add-ons |
| **Renewed without negotiating** | CRM | See clear value | Upsell on renewal |
| **Requested feature on higher tier** | Support tickets | Wants more capability | Tier upgrade |
| **Company raised funding** | External signals | Budget increased | Full expansion conversation |
| **New department engagement** | Product analytics | Multi-team expansion | Multi-seat / enterprise |

### Step 1.2: Expansion Readiness Score

| Factor | Score (1-5) | Weight |
|--------|:-----------:|:------:|
| **Product adoption** (are they using what they have?) | | 30% |
| **Account health** (NPS, support tickets, engagement) | | 25% |
| **Usage growth** (trending up?) | | 20% |
| **Business signals** (growth, funding, hiring) | | 15% |
| **Relationship strength** (champion engaged?) | | 10% |

**Score >3.5:** Active expansion outreach
**Score 2.5-3.5:** Nurture with value content
**Score <2.5:** Focus on adoption and health first

## Phase 2: Expansion Campaign Types

### Campaign 1: Tier Upgrade

**Trigger:** Usage approaching limits or advanced feature usage

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Target:** | Customers on lower tier showing power-user behavior |
| **Message:** | "You're outgrowing [current tier]. Here's what unlocks next." |
| **Proof:** | Case study of customer who upgraded and saw [result] |
| **Incentive:** | Free trial of next tier for 14 days, or first month at current price |

**Email Sequence:**

| Email | Day | Subject | Content |
|:-----:|:---:|---------|---------|
| 1 | 0 | "You're using [X]% of your [limit]" | Data-driven, show them their usage approaching ceiling |
| 2 | 5 | "What [Customer Name] unlocked when they upgraded" | Case study, social proof from similar customer |
| 3 | 10 | "Try [next tier] free for 14 days" | No-risk trial offer |
| 4 | 20 | "Your trial expires in 3 days — here's what you'd lose" | Loss aversion, recap of value experienced |

### Campaign 2: Feature Adoption → Expansion

**Trigger:** Low adoption of features available on current plan

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Target:** | Customers not using key features they're already paying for |
| **Message:** | "You're leaving value on the table. Here's how to get more from [Product]." |
| **Goal (first):** | Drive adoption of current features |
| **Goal (second):** | Once adopted, present the natural next step (expansion) |

**Content Sequence:**

| Piece | Format | Content |
|:-----:|--------|---------|
| 1 | Email | "3 features you're not using (but should be)" |
| 2 | In-app | Guided tour of underused features |
| 3 | Webinar | "Power user workshop: Get 3x more from [Product]" |
| 4 | Email | "Now that you're using [feature], here's the next level" |

### Campaign 3: Seat/Team Expansion

**Trigger:** Multiple users from same company or team growth signals

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Target:** | Accounts with growing teams or new department interest |
| **Message:** | "[Product] works even better when the whole team uses it" |
| **Incentive:** | Volume discount, team onboarding session |

### Campaign 4: Cross-Sell (Add-On / New Product)

**Trigger:** Customer buying one product could benefit from another

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Target:** | Customers of Product A who have use cases for Product B |
| **Message:** | "You're already [doing X]. Here's how to also [achieve Y]." |
| **Bridge:** | Show the natural connection between products |

## Phase 3: Expansion Communication

### The Expansion Conversation Framework

**For account managers and customer success:**

| Step | What to Do | Why |
|------|-----------|-----|
| 1. **Lead with value** | "I wanted to share something I noticed about your usage..." | Opens with insight, not a sales pitch |
| 2. **Show the gap** | "You're doing X really well. Most customers at your stage also..." | Creates awareness of the opportunity |
| 3. **Quantify the upside** | "Based on your usage, upgrading could [save/generate] [specific amount]" | Makes the value concrete |
| 4. **Offer a bridge** | "Want to try it for [free trial / reduced rate] before committing?" | Reduces risk |
| 5. **Set a timeline** | "Let's check back in [2 weeks] to see how it's working" | Creates natural follow-up |

### What NOT to Say in Expansion Conversations

| Don't | Do Instead |
|-------|-----------|
| "Would you like to upgrade?" | "Based on your usage, here's what you'd unlock..." |
| "We have a promotion right now..." | "Your account data shows you'd benefit from..." |
| "Lots of customers are upgrading..." | "[Specific similar customer] upgraded and saw [result]..." |
| Push during support issues | Wait until the issue is resolved + customer is satisfied |

## Phase 4: Measuring Expansion Success

### Expansion Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target | Current |
|--------|-----------|:------:|:-------:|
| **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** | (Revenue from existing customers at period end) / (Revenue at period start) | >110% | % |
| **Expansion MRR** | MRR added from upgrades, add-ons, seat expansion | $ | $ |
| **Expansion rate** | % of customers who expanded in the period | >15%/year | % |
| **Time to expansion** | Average months from initial purchase to first expansion | <12 mo | mo |
| **Upgrade conversion rate** | % of expansion campaign recipients who upgrade | >5% | % |
| **Cross-sell attach rate** | % of customers using 2+ products | >20% | % |

## Phase 5: Expansion Readiness Checklist

- [ ] Product usage data accessible and analyzed
- [ ] Expansion signals defined and tracked
- [ ] Customer health scores active in CRM
- [ ] Email sequences written and tested
- [ ] Sales / CS team briefed on expansion talk tracks
- [ ] Case studies available for expansion proof
- [ ] Reporting dashboard tracking NRR and expansion MRR
- [ ] Customer journey mapped: entry → adoption → expansion triggers
- [ ] Pricing for upgrades and add-ons documented
- [ ] Experiment framework applied (A/B test expansion messaging)
